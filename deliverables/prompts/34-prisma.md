# PRISMA — Festival of Living Light

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a one-page festival site called PRISMA — a light-art and projection festival — as a single self-contained HTML file with a raw WebGL GLSL kaleidoscope hero, no libraries and a single generated photograph. Palette: black velvet #0A090D background with jewel accents amethyst #9B4DCA, emerald #1FA97A, citrine #F5C542, sapphire #2E6BD6. Google Fonts: Monoton solely for the neon-deco hero word "PRISMA" (gradient-filled text with a slow animated hue-rotate), Outfit for everything else. Hero: full-viewport fragment shader that polar-folds the plane into N mirrored wedges (atan/mod/abs), where N is a float smoothly morphing between 6, 8 and 12 via pill buttons and a slow auto cycle; the wedge is filled with layered moving sine bands and fbm noise fields graded into the jewel palette over deep velvet shadows, citrine kept sparse as golden veins. Interaction: mouse angle rotates the fold axis, mouse distance from centre controls zoom, eased with frame-rate-independent smoothing and shortest-path angle interpolation; additionally a cursor-drawn white beam follows the pointer angle in unfolded space, progressively dispersing into the four jewel colours with radius like a live prism, brightening to full while the pointer is pressed and flaring with warm white light where it crosses the mirror seams. The hero headline gains a live chromatic dispersion fringe on hover via animated text-shadows. Sections: about with stat grid, three installation cards each showing a live 120px mini kaleidoscope (same shader, DPR 1, shared rAF loop, IntersectionObserver visibility gating, max three concurrent), a "last year" documentation section built on a generated 16:9 night photograph of four palette-coloured light beams in fog over a reflective plaza (1920×1072 WebP, lazy-loaded with real dimensions) revealed through a prism-split — three copies of the image reduced to pure R/G/B channels with inline SVG feColorMatrix filters and screen-blended over black (pure channels never overlap, so screen equals additive and the converged stack reconstructs the exact original), converging from ±3.2% horizontal offsets over the section's first third of scroll, then drifting on a slow Ken Burns scale 1→1.06 across the rest of its scroll life — an artists lineup grid, three ticket-night cards, footer. Cap hero DPR at 1.25 for 60fps. Honour prefers-reduced-motion with a frozen still frame, instant control steps, no beam, and a converged static photo with no drift. Keep horizontal scroll at zero on 390px screens (full-bleed stage uses width:100%, never 100vw). Copy should be electric but curated — a nocturnal turbine hall in Zürich, no lorem ipsum.

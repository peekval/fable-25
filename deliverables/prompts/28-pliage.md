# PLIAGE — Atelier de papier, Paris

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a one-page website for PLIAGE, a fictional Parisian origami atelier. Palette: paper white #FBF9F4, warm paper #F5F1E7, shadow gray #B8B2A6, one crane red #D9302F, graphite text #22201C. Fonts from Google: DM Serif Display for headlines, Nunito Sans for body. The signature is a real-time 3D origami hero built with three.js from a CDN: a square sheet of paper on a shadow-catching table, segmented into four triangular corner flaps, each parented to a hinge group whose origin and axis lie exactly on its crease line. Pin the stage and let scroll position scrub the folding bidirectionally - no triggers: the north-west corner valley-folds to the sheet's heart, the two side corners follow as a mirrored pair one breath apart, the last corner closes the blintz, and scrolling up unfolds everything. Give the paper procedural washi character: canvas-painted fibre textures, a warm-white front, each landed back one luminance step darker, lit by a soft studio key with shadow maps plus a cool fill (remember zero-thickness planes need shadowSide DoubleSide to cast). Draw dashed score lines onto the sheet just before each crease with a small red target dot, put a red square at the sheet's heart for the folds to swallow, and let free corners curl subtly toward the visitor's cursor like paper attracted by static. Between folds, ease the model through keyframed rotations so each crease faces the camera. For the finale, hand the closed packet off seamlessly to a segmented bird model that lifts off, folds up a neck, droops both wings about its spine and flicks its wingtips into a crane silhouette that gently beats its wings. Cap device pixel ratio at 2, pause the render loop off-screen and on hidden tabs, simplify shadows on phones, and keep a full CSS 3D fold rig with a low-poly SVG crane as the fallback for no WebGL, no JavaScript and prefers-reduced-motion, which shows the finished bird as a still. Sections: the folding hero with step captions and a progress rail, a philosophy-of-the-fold editorial with a pull quote and stats, a gallery of three slowly rotating polygon sculptures (crane, boat, fox) on cards with page-curl hover corners, atelier workshops on a graphite panel, and a footer. Add subtle SVG-turbulence paper grain and refined bilingual French-accented copy.

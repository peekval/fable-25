# 22 — FIELD NOTES · Portugal Travel Sketchbook

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML travel sketchbook titled "Field Notes — Portugal". Palette: warm paper #F8F4EA on a desk background #ECE4D2, pen ink #3A3733, Portuguese blue #2B4C7E, terracotta #D97D54, olive #8A9B6E, sky #7FA8C9. Fonts: Caveat (headlines + handwritten annotations), Patrick Hand (kickers, captions, page tabs), Lora (body prose). Signature technique: every illustration is hand-plotted inline SVG line art that draws itself — give each stroke pathLength="1" with stroke-dasharray:1 and dashoffset:1, then on IntersectionObserver reveal transition dashoffset to 0; measure each path's true length with getTotalLength to derive per-stroke duration and stagger, and compress the whole schedule so no page takes longer than ~4 seconds; soft ellipse colour washes, dashed route/beam lines and Caveat labels fade in after the linework. Present each chapter as a slightly rotated paper sheet with ruled or dotted notebook backgrounds, a corner-curl shadow, striped washi-tape strips cut with clip-path, rotated coloured page tabs, a circular airmail rubber stamp, and handwritten margin annotations with wobbly SVG arrows. Add an SVG feTurbulence paper-grain overlay and a pencil-dot cursor trail drawn from a pooled set of fading dots (fine pointers only). Second signature: tape three finished watercolour paintings into the pages as plates — tram 28 in an Alfama street (4:5), an azulejo corner study with margin swatch dabs (4:5), and a fishing harbour at golden hour (3:2) — generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP at 2K from watercolour-style prompts locked to the exact site palette, then optimised to WebP with ffmpeg (lazy-loaded, real width/height). Pin each plate with diagonal washi-tape corners or a pure-CSS paperclip on a paper mat, at a slight resting rotation. Reveal each plate with a watercolour bloom when scrolled into view: stack four soft-edged radial-gradient CSS mask layers on the image and animate their mask-size from 0% to ~340% at staggered rates while saturation and a slight blur resolve, so the picture spreads like pigment on wet paper; then a handwritten Caveat caption with a Portuguese date writes itself in via a clip-path wipe plus a self-drawing pencil underline (pathLength dash). On hover a plate lifts — straightens to 0°, rises a few pixels, shadow deepens — as if picked up. Give the sheets and plates subtle counter-rate scroll parallax using the CSS translate property (rAF-driven) so the rotate transforms are preserved. Pages: cover with suitcase sketch and flight route, tram 28 in Lisbon (+ tram plate), azulejo façade study in Porto (+ tile plate), coastal lighthouse in a gale, café still life with espresso and pastel de nata, closing envelope page (+ harbour plate by the prose about posting pages home). Copy tone: witty first-person travel-diary prose with self-deprecating margin notes. No photographs — SVG plus the three generated watercolours only. Under reduced motion or without JavaScript, show every drawing fully finished and every painting pre-revealed, with no parallax; keep the page free of horizontal overflow at 390px.

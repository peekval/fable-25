# ABYSSAL — Institute for Deep-Ocean Research

## Full Reproduction Prompt

The entire page is one continuous dive: scrolling descends from the surface to 4,000 m, with a fixed monospace depth meter counting down the metres and naming the zone (sunlight, twilight, midnight, abyss). Interpolate the background colour between zone stops as depth increases. Palette: abyss black-blue #030812, bioluminescent cyan #3DF5FF, violet glow #8A5CFF, pale specimen white #DCE9F0. Fonts: Spectral (light serif, scientific-poetic headlines) and Space Mono (depth readouts, labels).

A full-viewport canvas behind the content renders: drifting marine-snow particles whose density varies by zone and which streak upward while scrolling; godray light beams that fade out below ~400 m; a school of ~60 small boids fish that flee the cursor; two or three procedural jellyfish with sine-pulsing bézier bells and trailing tentacles using follow-through physics, glowing softly; and, in the deepest zone, an anglerfish silhouette whose glowing lure follows the cursor as the only light — a radial-gradient spotlight the black body occludes.

Wonder-filled but scientifically accurate copy. Mood: dark, quiet, luminous, vast. Keep it at 60fps: pre-rendered glow sprites, particle caps, capped pixel ratio, single animation loop, reduced-motion fallback.

# PARSEC — Mountain Observatory (Site 07)

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML showcase for PARSEC, a fictional public observatory at 2,742 m on Piz Larun. Palette: near-black ink #05070F, star-white #F2F6FF, cyan #6EE7F0, magenta #C77DFF; translucent glass cards rgba(9,13,26,.58) with 1px cyan-tinted borders and backdrop blur. Fonts (Google): Unbounded 300–900 for display, Inter for body, a system mono stack for coordinates and readouts. Signature technique: a fixed full-viewport canvas starfield of 800–1400 stars in three parallax layers (different drift speeds, sizes and alphas), each star twinkling via sine-modulated alpha; the cursor acts as a gentle gravity well whose pull strength eases in and out so stars never jump; rare shooting stars streak past with gradient tails. Cap devicePixelRatio at 2, pause rendering when the tab hides, and only re-seed stars on a real resize (ignore mobile URL-bar height changes). Second canvas: a code-drawn nebula — additive radial-gradient blobs in cyan and violet, bezier filaments, per-pixel noise grain and a vignette — painted once at half resolution and upscaled. Add a fixed telescope "focus ring" top-right whose arc fills with scroll progress while its tick ring rotates. Sections: full-height hero with gradient-clipped wordmark and a mono coordinates line, visit stat cards with count-up numbers, four constellation cards whose SVG star-lines draw themselves on hover via stroke-dashoffset, a "tonight" instrument panel of mono readouts with a live local-sidereal-time clock, the nebula deep-field feature, three CHF membership tiers, footer. Copy tone: poetic but precise scientific romance — "two vertical kilometres above the nearest streetlight." Honor prefers-reduced-motion with a static sky.

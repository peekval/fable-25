# 42 — SILICA · Inference, etched in silicon

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML showcase site for SILICA, a fictional AI-inference-chip company: "SILICA — Inference, etched in silicon". Aesthetic: futuristic-clean dark and surgical — near-black #0B0D10, panels #12151A, a silicon-iridescence duotone of violet #8B7CF6 and cyan #5EEAD4, electric lime #B8FF29 as a rare surgical accent, text #E7EAF0, dim #8B93A3. Fonts: Familjen Grotesk for display, Spline Sans Mono for labels, readouts and code. Terse, confident hardware copy throughout ("Data moves; nothing waits", "350 watts leave quietly").

Signature piece: a scroll-scrubbed exploded die. A 430vh hero with a sticky 100vh viewport holds five SVG layers — substrate with a 12x12 pad grid, 16 compute tiles on a shared violet-to-cyan gradient, an interconnect mesh of 24 edges plus a perimeter ring, six hatched SRAM banks, and a brushed heat spreader engraved "SILICA S1 · TESSERA" — stacked as flat planes in a preserve-3d container at rotateX(60deg) rotateZ(45deg) with NO perspective, so the projection stays orthographic and crisp. Generate all repetitive geometry in JS. Map scroll progress to five staggered windows; collapse gaps cumulatively bottom-up (z_i = sum of T + GAP*(1-ease_j) so the upper stack rides down as each layer seats) with a mild easeOutBack snap, counter-translating the stage by 0.4*totalZ to keep the stack centered. As each layer seats, a mono label with a leader line flies in, and a mono readout line reports "02 · INTERCONNECT — CLOSING / SEATED". At 90% the die goes live: heat spreader and SRAM ghost to x-ray opacity, pulses stream along every edge (duplicate pulse paths, pathLength 100, stroke-dasharray 9 91, animated stroke-dashoffset with per-edge duration and negative-delay jitter), hovering a compute tile lights its adjacent edges lime via a tile-to-edges map, and three hero stats count up mechanically with tabular-nums (1,860 TOPS · 48.2 TOPS/W · 38 ms). Drive the scrub with a dirty-flag rAF; pause all pulse animations when the hero leaves the viewport (IntersectionObserver) or the tab hides.

Then: an Architecture section — sticky isometric mini-stack of five CSS plates beside five scroll steps; the active step lifts its plate with a lime outline. A full-bleed die-shot section: a 1920x1080 macro photo of an iridescent silicon die (lazy, real dims) revealed by a lime scanline sweep synced to a clip-path inset wipe, with an engraved mono caption. Benchmarks vs previous gen: scaleX bars and count-ups (142 vs 51 tok/s; 48.2 vs 19.5 TOPS/W; 38 vs 210 ms TTFT) with lime delta chips. A developer section with a beautifully styled Python snippet (attach, load, stream — blinking lime cursor). Footer CTA "The stack is closed. Open a terminal." with a lime button. Fixed "← 25" pill top-left. prefers-reduced-motion: static assembled die, counters landed, no pulses or scan. Must run from file:// with zero console errors, no horizontal scroll at 390px, genuinely good on mobile.

# SUMI 墨 — Kyoto Ink Painting Studio

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a one-page site for "SUMI 墨", a fictional sumi-e studio in Kyoto, where every mark is drawn in the browser — no image files. Palette: warm washi paper #F5F0E6 (deeper #EDE6D6), near-black ink #1A1815 applied at 60/40/15/8% alphas, vermilion seal red #C73E2E. Fonts: Shippori Mincho for display and kanji, Zen Kaku Gothic New for body. Signature technique — a procedural canvas brush: sample quadratic Bézier paths into closely spaced "stations"; at each station lay an overlapping cluster of translucent dabs — one core blob, three dry-hair streaks offset along the path normal, and an occasional low-alpha bleed halo — so accumulated alpha reads as ink soaking into thirsty paper. Give every stroke a width profile with a firm entry, breathing middle and dry tapered exit, like a brush lifting off. Seed a deterministic PRNG (mulberry32-style) per stroke so a resize rebuilds identical marks. Use the brush for: an ensō circle drawn in one ~2-second breath around the hero 墨 on load; wide sweep and underline strokes that paint themselves as they scroll into view and never un-paint; and three complete algorithmic landscapes — misty ridges from layered sines filled with downward-fading gradients, mist bands, a leaning pine with needle flicks, a river boat with fisherman, red seal stamps — rendered offscreen and revealed like fog lifting. Add SVG-turbulence paper grain over the page, vertical-rl headings with kanji, an animated hanko stamp (scale-down slam, slight rotation, roughened edges), and generous yohaku whitespace. Sections: hero, philosophy, four-treasures grid with flick strokes, ink-wash gradient interlude, gallery, workshop details, footer. Tone: quiet, reverent, precise. Under reduced motion paint everything instantly.

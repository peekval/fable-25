# KURO — fourteen seats, one evening

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build KURO, a one-page site for a fictional 14-seat omakase restaurant, as a single self-contained HTML file (Google Fonts only; one local asset, assets/nigiri.webp). It must be the most restrained page imaginable: lacquer black #141210 (warm #1B1815), rice white #F3EEE4, nikiri brown #6B4A2E, cedar #8C6D4A, one ember accent #C25A3A, dim #7E766B. Fonts: Cormorant Garamond light with wide letter-spacing for Latin display, Shippori Mincho for body and for vertical-writing kanji accents (writing-mode: vertical-rl).

The page IS the tasting menu. Arrival: a full-viewport black screen where a vertical kanji phrase, the word KURO, and "Fourteen seats. One evening." breathe in through slow staggered opacity keyframes. Then eight courses (sakizuke/chawanmushi, hirame, nodoguro, toro, uni, anago, owan/dobin-mushi, kanmi), each an article 210vh tall holding a sticky 100svh moment: vertical kanji course number (第一品 · ichi), dish name huge and letterspaced, a Japanese sub-line, one reverent description of at most 20 words, a single ember dot. A rAF scroll loop maps each course's progress through two smoothsteps — fade in 6–34%, hold, fade out 72–98%, with a 26px settle — so there is a breath of pure black between courses. A fixed progress thread of eight thin segments on the right edge fills segment by segment as the meal advances; the photo course's segment is ember.

Atmosphere: a fixed canvas of ~26 rising steam wisps (16 mobile) — radial gradients stretched 1.9× vertically, sine sway, alpha ≤ .034. Each course carries data-heat (hot broths near 1, sashimi near 0); lerp the global steam intensity toward the visible course's heat so steam swells and stills with the menu. Cap DPR at 2, stop the loop on visibilitychange, remove the canvas under prefers-reduced-motion and render all courses statically. Section titles get a sumi ink underline: a tapered filled path revealed by a mask stroke animating stroke-dashoffset via IntersectionObserver.

Course four is the only photograph: generate a 4:5 macro of one toro nigiri on a black stone plate with a nikiri brush stroke and one steam wisp (Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP), finish with ffmpeg to a 1400×1750 WebP (~70KB), and reveal it full-viewport by a light-raise — scroll progress drives filter brightness(0→.96) saturate(.6→1) — with lazy loading, real dimensions, bottom scrim, caption in the lower third.

Then the counter: a top-down SVG of the hinoki slab (two quadratic arcs plus grain lines), an itamae circle, and 14 focusable seats placed along a Bézier; hover or focus shows that guest's ritual (arrival time, sake pairing, one habit) in an aria-live caption; seat 8 is ember, "yours", and clicking it eases to a minimal reservation: date pills (closed Sun/Mon, one night full), seat line, one "Hold my seat" button that simply holds — no backend. Fixed "← 25" pill to /, footer link to the build guide. Zero console errors over file://, scrollWidth ≤ 392 at 390px, changelog comment at the end.

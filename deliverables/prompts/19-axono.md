# AXONO — Architecture & Urban Design (Site 19)

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML site for AXONO, a Zürich studio for architecture and urban design. Palette: warm paper #F6F5F1, ink #23252B, drafting blue #2E5EAA, coral accent #FF6A5C, building faces #E9ECF3 / #D6DDEB. Fonts: Manrope (800 for tight headlines) plus JetBrains Mono for dimensions and labels; give the body a faint blueprint grid with crosshair ticks. Signature technique #1 — a runtime-generated isometric hero city: fourteen buildings defined purely as data (grid position, footprint, height, name, programme) are projected with true 30° axonometric math into SVG polygons (top, left and right faces plus faint facade floor lines), painter-sorted by depth; they drop in from above with a springy overshoot ease and 80ms stagger, and hovering or focusing one draws a leader line out to a mono spec label while dimension lines with arrowheads sketch themselves around the block. Signature technique #2 — the centerpiece: a real 3D axonometric tower rendered with three.js (0.147, from CDN) on a fixed full-viewport transparent canvas behind the page, through an OrthographicCamera locked to the classic 30° elevation / 45° azimuth drawing angle. Model the building as abstract architectural massing — a plinth, a two-level colonnaded podium, a twelve-storey slab-and-column tower with facade panels checkerboarded across the two visible faces, a grounded stair volume, a coral service core poking above a roof pavilion with parapets — every box flat-shaded with three fixed face tones (near-white top, the two facade greys) plus an EdgesGeometry wireframe in drafting blue, over a site grid of line segments. Scrub its assembly to scroll through the project index (narrow the index to a left column so the tower stands beside it): slabs glide in from alternating offsets, columns rise from their slabs, panels snap on, bottom-up with overlapping windows so scrolling backwards disassembles it. Hovering a project row lifts and coral-tints its storey band; horizontal pointer movement rotates the model ±8° with inertia. Signature technique #3 — a scroll-scrubbed "the plan stands up" 280vh pinned ink-dark section: a 1:100 floor-plan SVG group (walls, door swings, coral stair core) is interpolated via matrix() from identity to the isometric frame, then extruded — side walls hang from the transformed outline, a dashed footprint stays on the ground, and a live mono readout reports ELEV and PHASE (PLAN → AXONOMETRY → EXTRUSION → VOLUME). Follow it with a second pinned sheet, "EXPLODED AXONOMETRIC · SHEET A-21": the tower's storeys pull apart vertically along a dashed coral datum axis and reassemble, with live SEPARATION and STATE readouts; by the contact block the finished building settles small into the title-block corner and gently orbits. Add digit-roller counters for project stats and full-row hover inversion in the project index. Sections: hero headline with the city drawing, four-pass approach grid, project index of three works, the morph section, exploded sheet, contact with an oversized mailto, footer signed "DRAWN, NOT RENDERED." Copy: exacting Swiss-architect confidence. Performance and fallbacks: cap devicePixelRatio at 2, pause the render loop when the tower's sections are offscreen or the tab is hidden, simplify the massing at ≤768px and render the tower as a half-opacity watermark where text passes over it; if WebGL or the CDN fails, the page must collapse to the complete flat design with the 3D-only sections hidden; reduced motion unpins everything and shows the completed tower as a static frame.

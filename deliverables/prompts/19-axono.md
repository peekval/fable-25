# AXONO — Architecture & Urban Design (Site 19)

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML site for AXONO, a Zürich studio for architecture and urban design. Palette: warm paper #F6F5F1, ink #23252B, drafting blue #2E5EAA, coral accent #FF6A5C, building faces #E9ECF3 / #D6DDEB. Fonts: Manrope (800 for tight headlines) plus JetBrains Mono for dimensions and labels; give the body a faint blueprint grid with crosshair ticks. Signature technique #1 — a runtime-generated isometric city: fourteen buildings defined purely as data (grid position, footprint, height, name, programme) are projected with true 30° axonometric math into SVG polygons (top, left and right faces plus faint facade floor lines), painter-sorted by depth. On first view they drop in from above with a springy overshoot ease and 80ms stagger; hovering or focusing a building draws a leader line out to a mono spec label, and dimension lines with arrowheads sketch themselves around the block. Signature technique #2 — a scroll-scrubbed "the plan stands up" section: inside a 280vh pinned sticky stage, a 1:100 floor-plan SVG group (walls, door swings, coral stair core) is interpolated via matrix() from identity to the isometric frame, then extruded — side walls hang from the transformed outline, a dashed footprint stays on the ground, and a live mono readout reports ELEV and PHASE (PLAN → AXONOMETRY → EXTRUSION → VOLUME). Add digit-roller counters for project stats and full-row hover inversion in the project index. Sections: hero headline with the city drawing, four-pass approach grid, project index of three works, the morph section on ink-dark background, contact with an oversized mailto, footer signed "DRAWN, NOT RENDERED." Copy: exacting Swiss-architect confidence. Reduced motion: unpin the morph and show final states instantly.

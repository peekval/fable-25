# GRID° — Swiss Editorial Journal (Site 06)

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML site for GRID°, a fictional quarterly journal of systematic Swiss design, issue №07. Palette: paper #FAFAF8, ink #111111, and one accent red #E63329 applied surgically; hairlines at rgba(17,17,17,.18). Fonts (Google): Inter Tight 500–900 for display, Space Grotesk 400–700 for text and labels. Everything sits on a strict 12-column CSS grid (1440px max shell, 24px gutters) collapsing to 4 columns under 900px. Signature technique: a toggleable grid overlay — a fixed, full-height layer of twelve translucent red columns, off by default, switched by a corner button and the G key, exposing the layout system itself. Second signature: article index rows that invert on hover — a ::before ink bar scales from scaleY(0) to full height and bleeds the full viewport width via inset:0 calc(50% − 50vw), flipping text to paper white while the oversized index numeral turns red. Hero: three uppercase headline lines ("Order is / not a cage. / It is a choice"), each starting on a different grid column, one word in transparent stroke-outline; below, a flat geometric SVG composition of rules, blocks and circles. Sections: masthead with giant logo and issue meta, hero, six-essay article index (Müller-Brockmann, Frutiger, Aicher, Vignelli, Ruder, Gerstner), a dark manifesto marquee strip with a red top border, and an ink colophon footer listing typefaces and hexes. Copy tone: precise, factual design-history editorial — red is a scalpel, not a paintbrush. Stagger scroll reveals with IntersectionObserver, duplicate the marquee via aria-hidden clones, and disable all motion under prefers-reduced-motion.

The plate: after the essay index add SEC. B, "№07 in print" — one real photograph of the printed journal open on a white surface, generated with Nano Banana 2 (Higgsfield MCP, text-to-image at 2K) from a palette-locked prompt (paper #FAFAF8, ink #111111, exactly one red rule #E63329; rigorous greeked 12-column spread, matte uncoated stock, no hands, 3:2), compressed to WebP with ffmpeg (`assets/issue07.webp`, `loading="lazy" decoding="async"`, intrinsic 1600×1074). Mount it exactly on the site grid (columns 1–8, a caption column at 10–13) inside crop marks and registration marks drawn by the page itself — CSS hairlines offset 18px outside the trim with a 4px gap, plus two SVG circle-crosshair registration marks — over a figcaption set in the journal's label system. Reveal it mechanically, never a fade: slice the photo into a 6×4 matrix of tiles (background-size:600% 400%, per-tile background-position) that snap on in strict reading order at ~34ms per cell, a red-outlined "hot" cell tracking the head, empty cells showing as hairline grid until filled, ending with a rotated red "PASSED FOR PRESS" stamp; the lazy `<img>` remains the semantic content and appears assembled and static under prefers-reduced-motion or without JS. Then escalate the grid twice more: SEC. C, "the grid re-divides" — a 340vh section with a sticky bordered stage whose internal grid hard-snaps (no tweening) through ÷1 ÷2 ÷3 ÷4 ÷6 ÷12 divisions as scroll progress crosses each sixth, an ink block, red block and specimen paragraph reflowing to new column spans at every snap, with a tabular counter ("12 ÷ 4") and a tick row marking the current division (static at ÷12 under reduced motion). And give the grid toggle a "show construction" superpower: the overlay columns gain counters 01–12, red crop marks appear in the viewport corners, a fixed spec readout states shell/columns/gutter/margins, and the plate annotates its own column span, trim size and 3∶2 ratio.

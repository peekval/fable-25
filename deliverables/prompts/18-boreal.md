# BOREAL — Slow Arctic Expeditions (Site 18)

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML site for BOREAL, a slow-travel expedition outfit above the Arctic Circle. Palette: polar night #0B1026, aurora green #6BFFB0, aurora violet #9D7BFF, snow #EAF2F6, ember orange #FF9E6B, hairlines rgba(234,242,246,.14). Fonts: Fraunces (light 300, optical sizing) for serif display, Outfit 300 for body, IBM Plex Mono for data labels. Signature technique — a generative aurora canvas: four ribbons, each drawn as a closed shape whose top edge is a sum of three sines (two spatial frequencies plus slow horizontal drift) and whose bottom edge hangs a modulated curtain-length below; fill each with a vertical hsla gradient whose hue oscillates between 150 (green) and 262 (violet), composite with globalCompositeOperation "lighter", and render each ribbon twice — one wide soft glow pass, one bright pass (skip the soft pass under 640px). Behind it, a twinkling rectangle starfield; in front, three parallax SVG mountain layers moving at different scroll factors. Fix three full-screen gradient scenes behind the page and crossfade night → blue hour → ember as scroll passes the expeditions and cabin sections. The itinerary rail is a gently wobbling SVG path that draws itself via scroll-scrubbed stroke-dashoffset, with dots igniting green per day. Sections: hero (wordmark with green glow, live-data chips, scroll cue), philosophy with three tenets, three expedition rows with mono metadata (latitude, duration, season, typical low, price), six-day timeline, cabin-night section with a flickering radial fireglow and guest quote, departures list plus understated enquiry form, quiet footer. Copy voice: unhurried, literary, honest. Pause the canvas when off-screen; reduced motion gets one static painted frame and instant reveals.

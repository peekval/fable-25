# STEEP — A Tea House for Slow Hours

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a one-page website for STEEP, a modern tea house and ceremony studio. Palette: celadon #B7C9B1, cream porcelain #F6F1E7, deep tea #4A3728, matcha #7A9B5E. Fonts from Google: Cormorant Infant for headings, Karla for body. The hero is an interactive fluid simulation of tea steeping into clear water: a pool of roughly 3000 particles (half on mobile) advected by an analytic curl-noise velocity field, rendered as short soft strokes onto a canvas that is never cleared — fade it each frame with a translucent cream fill so trails accumulate into ink-like diffusion. Moving the cursor pours tea-brown dye along its path, inheriting stroke momentum; when idle, auto-drips fall every few seconds so the water stays alive. The giant serif title STEEP sits inside the fluid: subtract a blurred glyph mask from the dye each frame so colour flows around and behind the letters. Cap DPR at 2, use a single requestAnimationFrame loop, pause when hidden, and draw one static pre-bloomed swirl for prefers-reduced-motion. Below the hero: a philosophy section with a large italic pull-quote, three tea cards with hand-drawn porcelain-cup SVGs and animated bezier steam wisps, a horizontal ceremony timeline (0:30, 1:00, 2:00) whose gradient fill animates on scroll, and a quiet booking section on deep-tea brown. Between the timeline and the booking, place one contemplative portrait figure (~520px, centred): a ~4.5-second seamless video loop of steam rising from a matcha bowl in celadon glaze on cream linen with a bamboo whisk — generate the still with Nano Banana 2 (text-to-image, 2K) in the exact site palette, animate only the steam with Kling 3.0 Turbo (image-to-video from that still), then finish with ffmpeg: WebP poster, h264 at CRF 26, and a crossfade of the clip onto its own head so the loop never visibly restarts. Give the video autoplay muted loop playsinline preload="metadata" with the poster, pause it off-screen via IntersectionObserver, never autoplay under prefers-reduced-motion (poster only), and caption it with a thin celadon rule, one italic serif line, and a small uppercase label. Mood: calm, precise, ceremonial — generous whitespace, slow easings, nothing loud.

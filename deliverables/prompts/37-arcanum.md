# ARCANUM — A Trading Card Game You Can Almost Touch

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file page for ARCANUM, a fictional trading card game whose featured cards are true holographic foils rendered in WebGL. Palette: midnight #0E0B18 background, card-frame gold #C9A227, mist lavender #B9B4CC body text, near-white #EFEAF7 headings, and a vivid iridescent rainbow gradient (pink, amber, yellow, mint, cyan, violet) reserved for foil. Fonts: Cinzel Decorative for the logotype and card titles, Sora for UI and body. Hero: the ARCANUM wordmark filled with the rainbow gradient above three collectible cards — a golden-green ouroboros serpent sigil (rare), a moonlit indigo tower (epic), and a flame-winged phoenix (mythic, center) — each drawn as elegant inline vector art, summoned on load with a rising flip, a foil flash and a burst of rainbow sparks. Card rendering: give every card its own small WebGL canvas behind its HTML frame and text. In the fragment shader, draw the art window from two textures made by splitting the card's SVG into a background plane and a subject plane (rasterize with overscan bleed), sample them at different parallax offsets driven by tilt, and let the subject cast a soft drop shadow — the DOM frame acts as the nearest third layer. Over that, composite a procedural holo-foil pattern that differs per rarity — scallop-rimmed scales for the serpent, radial moonbeam rays for the tower, a full-art galaxy swirl for the phoenix — colored by a cosine-palette thin-film rainbow whose phase is keyed to the tilt vector so the hue truly shifts with viewing angle, gated to ignite as the card leans, and masked by the subject's alpha so the creature stays in front of the shimmer. Add hash-cell sparkle glints thresholded on a per-cell random angle so individual sparkles pop in and out while wiggling, plus a glossy specular streak (CSS overlay) sweeping with the pointer. Tilt: pointer position maps to rotateX/rotateY under perspective through an under-damped spring for real cardstock inertia; bind pointer events to an untransformed wrapper so the tilted card never slips out from under the cursor; on touch, tilt from a drag and fall back to a gentle auto-sway or device orientation. Second act: a sealed iridescent booster pack that tears open on click along a jagged clip-path seam, releasing a golden burst and dealing the three cards into a centered fan with springy overshoot, guilloché-rosette card backs flipping face-up in sequence, their WebGL contexts booting lazily at the tear. Add a mythic-but-witty lore section, four expansion sets, rarity tiers (Ash, Ember, Gilded, Arcanum) with gem badges, and a footer. Engineering bar: cap devicePixelRatio at 2, pause rendering for offscreen cards via IntersectionObserver and when the tab is hidden, drop a sparkle layer at or below 768px, keep pure-CSS foil layers as the no-WebGL fallback, honor prefers-reduced-motion with static flattering angles and zero loops, animate only transform and opacity, and allow no page errors and no horizontal overflow at 390px.

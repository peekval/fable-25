# 25 — SEED · Generative Gallery, Edition of One

## Full Reproduction Prompt

Build a single-file HTML generative-art gallery called "SEED — an exhibition, edition of one". Gallery aesthetic: warm wall #F5F5F2, ink #1C1C1C with softer alpha tints for secondary text, hairline rules everywhere. Fonts: Libre Caslon Text (serif body, italic artwork titles) with Space Grotesk for small tracked-out uppercase labels. Signature technique: a seeded PRNG (mulberry32, seeded from arrival time XOR Math.random) drives every decision, so reloading with the same seed would reproduce each work brushstroke for brushstroke; stamp the visit as "Edition #XXXX" (seed mod 10000) in a fixed corner tag beside the raw seed, and give each of the four works its own derived 32-bit seed printed on its plate caption. Render four canvas artworks with zero images and zero libraries: (1) a two-octave flow field — ~1700 fine mist particles with finite lifespans stroking low-alpha segments along summed sin/cos vector angles at two scales, the whole field coiled around one hidden eddy that adds quadratically-stronger rotation near its core, plus ~130 wider ribbon strokes whose width tapers over each life and which occasionally drop small ink-seed dots; (2) recursive rectangle subdivision filled as soft-edged Rothko-like colour fields using ~14 jittered low-alpha passes per block; (3) concentric rings whose radii wobble with three random sine harmonics — hand-unsteady by design; (4) ~300 bodies given tangential starting velocity around hidden gravity attractors, tracing coiled orbital threads. Choose one of five curated palettes per visit (e.g. Ink & Vermillion, Dusk, Terracotta & Teal, Moss & Ochre, Plum & Ash) and generate titles from shuffled adjective+noun lists. Growth, not paint-bucket: each work grows over ~2.5 seconds (150 requestAnimationFrame frames, target-step pacing so all algorithms finish together) when scrolled into view; pause the growth loop when the canvas leaves the viewport and resume on return (IntersectionObserver storing the loop continuation). Clicking a canvas — or a "Replant" button on its museum label — retires that single work's seed and regrows it alone. Hang the works in white frames with hover lift, radial spotlight glows above (a 150%-wide ::before inside an overflow-x:clip gallery) that subtly track the cursor via a per-work --spot-x custom property with a ~1s eased transition (pointer:fine only), and museum labels: roman numeral, italic title, algorithm name, medium line, pixel dimensions plus the work's seed. Add an "Acquire" button that springs in a classic red sold-dot and writes a certificate numbered with that work's seed (replanting dismantles the acquisition); a Regenerate button (plus the R key) that fades the wall, retires the edition and mints a new show; and a colophon counting "decisions spent". Copy tone: dry, honest wall text — "arithmetic wearing good light". Reduced motion: render instantly, no growth, no lifts or fades, no spotlight tracking.

The Riot Paper Manifesto

(v13 — the first 12 were too polite)
  1. We believe anything worth saying is worth saying slightly too loud, slightly too close to your face, on paper that jams the printer.
  2. Perfection is a scam invented by people who sell rulers. Our margins are crooked on purpose. (The first three times were accidents. Now it's a style.)
  3. If it can't be made with scissors, glue, rage, and a photocopier with a personal grudge against us, we ask: does it really need to exist?
  4. We do not accept sponsors. We once accepted a sandwich, and the ethics committee (Deb) is still reviewing it.
  5. Every algorithm wants you calm, smooth, and scrolling. We want you awake, annoyed, and folding paper. One of these builds a scene. The other builds a feed.
  6. Noise is not the opposite of signal. Noise is signal that refused to fill out the form.
— the collective (present members: 5, feuding: 2)

The sun drives the page

HELIOS is a fictional solar-thermal startup. Its signature technique is a unified light system: one scroll-derived value (dawn → noon → dusk) that every visual on the page obeys at once.

01One number, one sun

Scroll progress maps to a day: p = scrollY / maxScroll, elevation elev = sin(pπ). A single rAF loop eases p toward its target and writes CSS custom properties on :root:

  • Light temperature — three keyed palettes (cool dawn #E3EAF0, white noon #F7F8F6, golden dusk #F5E2BE) interpolated in JS and written to --tint / --sky, so the whole page's ambient color follows the sun.
  • One global shadow vector — --sdx/--sdy/--slen/--shadow-c. Every card, pill and panel uses the same box-shadow variables, so shadows lengthen, rotate away from the sun and warm toward amber at dusk, coherently.
  • The sun element — a fixed radial-gradient disc translated along a viewport arc (low left → high center → low right).
  • Telemetry HUD — solar time (05:30–19:30), elevation in degrees, and field output MWt = 210 · elev^1.15, live in a fixed pill.

The loop idles after ~30 settled frames, stops on visibilitychange, and skips canvas work when it scrolls offscreen (IntersectionObserver). DPR is capped at 2. prefers-reduced-motion freezes everything at noon defaults declared in CSS.

02The heliostat canvas

A top-down field diagram: ~450 mirrors placed on flattened concentric ellipses around a receiver tower. The physics is real heliostat logic — each mirror's normal bisects the angle between the sun's diagram position and the tower, and the drawn stroke is perpendicular to that normal. Angles ease with a += Δ·0.09 per frame (shortest-arc lerp), which produces the satisfying sweep when you scroll fast. Mirrors gain a gold catch-light and sampled sun→mirror→tower rays appear as elevation rises; the receiver glow scales with output.

03The asset

assets/field.webp (1920×1080) — aerial golden-hour heliostat field in concentric arcs, generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP at 2K, palette-locked to the site's gold/warm-sand tones, then converted with ffmpeg to WebP. It lands as the "proof of reality" moment: revealed by a thin gold mirror-sweep line (a clip-path: inset() transition chased by a glowing 2px bar), loading="lazy" with real dimensions to avoid layout shift.

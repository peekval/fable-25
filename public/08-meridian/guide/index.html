How It Was Built

The Craft

The lobby is lit by a raw-WebGL atmosphere canvas — no library — composited additively between the CSS background and the type. A fullscreen shader pass renders three depth layers of diagonal volumetric light shafts, each a sum of Gaussian band profiles across the beam-perpendicular axis modulated by fbm noise that streams slowly along the beam, with a faint art-deco sunburst of cosine-power rays parallaxing deeper in the scene and an elliptical guard dimming the light behind the title so the type stays crisp. Through the beams drift some 2,400 CPU-simulated gold motes (900 on phones) drawn as depth-of-field point sprites — nearer motes larger and softer — whose brightness is masked by the very same GLSL shaft function, so the dust glitters only where light falls; the pointer swirls nearby motes with a decaying tangential impulse and they resettle into their drift, while scrolling eases the camera for parallax. The loop pauses whenever the hero leaves the viewport or the tab hides, devicePixelRatio is capped at 2, and static CSS gradient shafts stand in when WebGL is absent or motion is reduced. Around it, the original craft remains: every golden headline is a background-clipped linear gradient that slowly sheens across the type; the art-deco emblems are inline SVGs that draw themselves stroke by stroke via pathLength=1, stroke-dashoffset transitions and per-stroke CSS-variable delays; navigation is a fixed brass elevator panel scroll-spied by a −42%/−52% rootMargin IntersectionObserver; a faint feTurbulence film grain flickers over conic-gradient ray patterns that parallax at rAF-gated compositor speed; and the wide-tracked Poiret One headings stay optically centered through matching text-indent compensation.

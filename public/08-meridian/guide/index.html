How It Was Built

The Craft

Every golden headline in The Meridian is a background-clipped linear gradient — bronze into gold into pale gold and back — that slowly sheens across the type, so the lettering catches light like leaf under a chandelier. The art-deco emblems are inline SVGs that draw themselves: each ray, arc and keystone diamond carries pathLength=1 with a stroke-dashoffset transition and its own CSS-variable delay, so the sunburst assembles stroke by stroke as it scrolls into view, fills fading in last. Navigation is a fixed brass elevator panel whose round floor buttons glow gold when lit, driven by a scroll-spy IntersectionObserver tuned with a −42%/−52% rootMargin band so the correct floor is always illuminated. A faint feTurbulence film grain flickers over everything while conic-gradient ray patterns parallax behind the hero and bar at rAF-gated compositor speed. The wide-tracked Poiret One headings stay optically centered through matching text-indent compensation, and prefers-reduced-motion freezes the grain, pre-draws every ornament and drops the parallax entirely.

MAGNITUDE

A museum of scale — one scroll from the cosmic web to a single atom.

How it was built

The dive is a nested-scale handoff: seven scenes — cosmic web, galaxy, solar system, planet surface, leaf, cell, atom — each drawn entirely in code on its own oversized layer, stacked inside one sticky full-screen stage. Scroll position maps to a continuous progress value; the current scene scales smoothly from 1× up to about 18× while the next scene fades and grows out of the exact screen centre, so the end of one zoom is pixel-for-pixel the beginning of the next. Every scene plants a small "seed" of its successor at that centre point — a bright knot in the web, a lone star on a galaxy arm, a teal planet on an orbit ring — which is what makes the seams feel inevitable rather than stitched. A strict two-active-layers rule keeps it at 60 fps: only the outgoing and incoming scenes exist in the render tree at any moment, animated with transforms and opacity alone, and each layer wears a soft radial mask so it materialises as a glowing disc instead of a hard rectangle. A fixed logarithmic readout interpolates the exponent between each level's anchor scale, ticking from 10²⁶ m down to 10⁻¹⁰ m, while a slim magnitude rail tracks the seven floors of the museum. Under prefers-reduced-motion the zoom becomes a stepped, level-by-level crossfade.

Reproduce it

Build a single-page "museum of scale" website called MAGNITUDE: one continuous scroll-driven infinite zoom from the cosmic web down to a single atom, through seven nested levels — cosmic web filaments, spiral galaxy, solar system, planet-surface ridges, leaf with branching veins, living cell with soft organelles, atom with tilted electron orbitals. Use a full-screen stage pinned while a very tall section scrolls. Every level is its own code-drawn layer (particles, curves, gradients — no images). As the user scrolls, the current layer scales continuously from 1 up to roughly 18 while the next layer fades and grows in from the exact screen centre, each scene hiding a small seed of the next at that centre so the handoffs feel seamless; keep only two adjacent layers rendered at once, animate with transforms and opacity only, and give each layer a soft radial edge mask. Add a fixed logarithmic scale readout ticking from 10^26 m to 10^-10 m, a thin right-hand magnitude rail with seven markers, and one floating caption card per level carrying a short, accurate science fact. Palette: indigo night #0D1024 base, bone #EFECE6 UI, per-level accents #7B6CFF, #FFD27A, #2EC4B6, #6FBF4E, #E76A8B, #46E3E3. Fonts: Zilla Slab for display, IBM Plex Sans for UI. Include an intro hero, an epilogue ("you are here") with a log-scale ruler marking the human position, and a stepped crossfade fallback for reduced motion. Mood: quiet, awe-driven, science-museum precision.

MONSOON

Three weathers · recorded in ink & water

I · Rain

the downpour

The sky rehearses its argument all afternoon, then loses its temper at once. Rain writes fast cursive down the windows, signing everything. Streets fill with small applause. Somewhere a gutter learns to sing. We count the seconds between light and thunder, and call that distance weather, or luck.

18:42 — pressure falling · 34 mm before midnight

II · Fog

the white hour

Morning arrives without edges. The fog moves in like a landlord, measuring everything, naming nothing. Trees become rumors of trees; the harbor forgets its boats. Sound travels farther than sight, and softer. Walk out far enough and you become a rumor too — a grey coat, a slower kind of breathing.

06:15 — visibility 40 m · the harbor bell, twice

III · Clearing

the golden hour

Then, absolution. Light leans through the torn cloud like a ladder set down for whoever still needs one. Dust rises to meet it, each mote a small planet with its own gold season. The wet world steams. Everything that survived the storm now glitters, briefly, as if chosen.

16:03 — pressure rising · steam off the roof tiles

Monsoon

A weather journal in three movements, drawn live on canvas — no photographs, no film, no images at all. Set in Newsreader & Inter. Barometer readings are honest about the sky and unreliable in all other matters.

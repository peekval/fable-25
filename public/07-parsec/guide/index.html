How PARSEC was built

The instruments

The whole night sky is a single fixed canvas: between 800 and 1,400 stars are seeded into three parallax layers with different drift speeds and sizes, each one twinkling through a sine-modulated alpha, and the cursor acts as a gravity well whose pull strength fades in and out so stars never lurch when the pointer enters or leaves. Performance is the quiet star of the show — stars are sorted by color so the render loop changes fillStyle only three times per frame, pixel ratio is capped at 2, rendering pauses when the tab hides, and the field only re-seeds on a genuine resize so mobile URL-bar collapse never scrambles the sky. The nebula is painted entirely in code, once, at half resolution: additive radial-gradient blobs, bezier filaments, per-pixel grain and a vignette, upscaled into softness. A telescope "focus ring" in the corner doubles as a scroll-progress dial, constellation cards draw their star-lines on hover via stroke-dashoffset, the readout panel counts its numbers up on entry, and a real local-sidereal-time clock ticks in the header of the instrument feed. Reduced-motion visitors get a still, pre-drawn sky.

The sky also misbehaves on schedule: every 8–15 seconds a meteor streaks across the starfield — a radial-gradient bright head trailing a gradient tail, falling left or right at random — and one panel in the catalogue holds "the Surveyor" (Mensor), a fictional constellation of a theodolite on its tripod whose lines draw themselves in four staggered stages when scrolled into view, labelled in the site's mono style (α MEN · THE EYEPIECE, δ MEN · THE PLUMB). Under prefers-reduced-motion the meteors never fire and the Surveyor arrives pre-drawn.

Generated assets

The full-bleed station photograph — the dome on the Piz Larun ridge under the Milky Way, one warm light in the slit — was generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP at 2K, palette-locked to the site's four colours (#05070F, #F2F6FF, #6EE7F0, #C77DFF), then converted and compressed to WebP with ffmpeg (1920×1072, lazy-loaded with real dimensions). On the page it drifts with a subtle transform-based parallax and carries a low-density live canvas of twinkling stars over its sky, so the photograph and the code-drawn sky share one atmosphere. Reduced-motion visitors get the still image.

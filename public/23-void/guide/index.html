01 — How it was built

Everything on VOID ATELIER reveals itself the way light enters a room: elements begin fully hidden under clip-path insets and ease open over 1.3 seconds with a long architectural bezier, staggered and triggered exactly once by an IntersectionObserver. Hairline rules grow from zero width the same way, so the page appears to be drafted rather than loaded.

The black light-study section contains no image at all — the concrete room corner is three CSS gradients and a seam, crossed by a blurred, clip-path-trimmed light beam that pivots from an unseen window over 36 seconds while a small clock counts 06:00 to 18:00 in sync. Custom SVG data-URI cursors (a crosshair, a surveyor’s circle on links) and a press-G six-column grid overlay in difference blend-mode complete the drafting-table feel. The strict six-value grey palette, Inter at weight 200 and one italic serif are the entire visual budget; under reduced motion the beam freezes mid-afternoon and every reveal is disabled.

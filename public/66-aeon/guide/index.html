AEON — One valley, 4.6 billion years

How this experience works — and the prompt that rebuilds it. All of it is code: no image or audio assets, everything procedural.

01 One continuous shot

A full-viewport canvas landscape — sky, ridge, valley floor, foreground — that morphs through Earth's eras as you scroll, never cutting: Hadean magma seas, Archean ocean, Carboniferous swamp forest, Permian desert, Cretaceous river plain with a distant sauropod, ice age, Holocene grassland, the present city at dusk, rewilded ruins, and finally the red-giant sun. Transitions are believable in-between states driven by one damped scroll value.

02 The timeline instrument

A fixed logarithmic time readout (−3.8 Ga · Archean) scrubs with scroll; tick marks compress toward the present. Clicking any era fast-travels smoothly.

03 Witness moments

Each era carries one field-note caption ('The first rain falls for a million years.'). In the present-day city, a single window blinks — the visitor's.

04 Pacing & fallbacks

~1400vh of scroll, breathing grain whose rhythm slows in deep time; reduced motion swaps to era stills with a clickable timeline; layers thin on mobile; the painter pauses offscreen and on hidden tabs.

Reproduction prompt
Build a single-file deep-time experience called "AEON — One valley. 4.6 billion years." A full-viewport canvas paints ONE landscape (layered painterly rendering: sky gradient, distant ridge, mid valley, foreground) that morphs continuously through Earth's history as the visitor scrolls (~1400vh total): Hadean glowing magma seas with meteor streaks -> Archean ocean with volcanic islands -> Carboniferous swamp forest silhouettes -> Permian desert -> Cretaceous river plain with a slowly walking sauropod silhouette -> glacial valley -> Holocene grassland with one campfire light -> the present: a small city grid glowing at dusk (one window blinks) -> rewilded ruins -> a red giant filling the sky. Never cut: eras blend through transitional states (ocean recedes, forest rises, ice melts) driven by ONE damped scroll value. Fix a logarithmic timeline instrument at the bottom: mono era readout (e.g. "-90 Ma · CRETACEOUS"), log-compressed tick marks, click-to-travel. Each era shows one short field-note caption in italic serif. Add a breathing grain/vignette whose rhythm slows in ancient eras. prefers-reduced-motion: static era stills with instant timeline swaps. Pause painting offscreen/hidden; thin the layers on mobile; no horizontal overflow at 390px; zero console errors. Palette shifts per era but text stays bone-white with one cyan accent (#7FA8C9); typography: quiet mono + an editorial serif for captions.

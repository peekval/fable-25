Form Folgt.

01How it was built

FORM FOLGT is a fictional centenary exhibition site for the Bauhaus building in Dessau, set entirely in Archivo and Work Sans on warm paper with the classic red, blue and yellow primaries. The hero is a choreographed SVG composition: two black rules draw themselves in, a red circle glides in from far off-canvas, a blue square rotates home with a springy overshoot, and a yellow triangle drops last — and the whole sequence replays every time you scroll back to the top. The centerpiece is section 02, "Das Gebäude": a duotone archival photograph of the workshop wing with the vertical BAUHAUS lettering, mounted like an exhibition plate under a giant red catalogue number 001. Three shutter panels in red, blue and yellow cover the plate and slide-rotate out of the frame the first time you scroll to it — a pure CSS-transform reveal fired once by a class toggle, with a hard staggered bezier. Beside it sits the "Fassaden-Partitur", a kinetic Moholy-Nagy-style band whose circle, square, triangle and bar are driven by scroll progress quantized to eight steps: the circle translates along the grid, the square rotates in 45-degree increments, the triangle counter-translates and flips, the bar grows — every change snapping mechanically, never floating. Further down, a kinetic "stage" section pays homage to Oskar Schlemmer: four shapes fall under real requestAnimationFrame gravity, bounce with restitution, then snap their rotation onto a 90-degree grid before the loop shuts itself off. Ticket cards hard-swap to full red, blue or yellow color blocks on hover with a two-frame steps() transition. Everything degrades gracefully: without JavaScript or with reduced motion, the shutters sit open, the score sits composed, the physics stage becomes a pre-composed still life and all reveals are simply visible.

Generated assets. The archival plate (assets/dessau.webp, 1200×1490) was generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP from a 2K duotone prompt — Bauhaus Dessau workshop wing with the vertical facade lettering, halftone grain, ink #141414 on paper #F4F1EA — then converted and optimized to WebP with ffmpeg. It ships with intrinsic dimensions and lazy loading.

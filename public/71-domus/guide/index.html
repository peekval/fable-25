DOMUS — Seehaus Meridian

A house you can scroll. How the 80-frame walkthrough works — and the prompt that rebuilds the whole site.

01 The frame scrub

The Rundgang is a 560vh wrapper with a sticky 100svh stage. A full-viewport canvas scrubs 80 WebP frames (1024×576) of one continuous steadicam take: scroll progress inside the wrapper maps to a target frame, and a damped playhead (lerp 0.28 per rAF tick) chases it — fast enough to feel direct, damped enough to feel like walking, not seeking. 2% dead zones at both ends hold frame 1 and frame 80 still while the stage docks and undocks. Drawing is hybrid-fit: full cover on landscape, width-filling with a gentle ~1.45× side-crop on portrait phones, so mobile never gets a letterbox sliver.

02 Loading without a stutter

Nothing loads until an IntersectionObserver (600px rootMargin) sees the section coming. Frames are then requested in an interleaved order — every 8th frame first, then every 4th, 2nd, 1st — so a visitor who scrolls straight into the middle finds a nearby frame already decoded; draw() falls back to the nearest loaded neighbour. A quiet umber veil ("Rundgang wird geladen") sits over the stage until frame 1 decodes, then fades.

03 Room captions, pinned to frames

Captions are bound to frame ranges verified by eye against the extracted stills, then re-checked in headless screenshots at four scroll depths. Each caption carries the room, the m², and one line of copy (de-CH, kein Eszett).

04 The floorplan progress line

Progress through the house is drawn as a minimal floorplan polyline — the actual path: entrée → wohnraum → treppe → küche → terrasse — revealed via stroke-dashoffset, with a dot positioned by getPointAtLength and room ticks that turn oak as you pass them. Under 560px it yields to a slim oak progress bar along the top of the stage.

05 Accessibility & fallbacks

prefers-reduced-motion removes the scrub entirely and serves five static stills (frames 8, 28, 42, 56, 75) stacked with the same captions; the hero CTA retargets to them. The whole page runs from file:// with zero console errors, no horizontal overflow at 390px, DPR capped at 2.

06 Asset pipeline

The walkthrough is one generated take: Kling 3.0 Turbo, a 10-second steadicam walkthrough prompt via the Higgsfield MCP, exploded with ffmpeg fps=8 into 80 WebP frames at 1024×576. The hero facade (1920×1080, golden hour, infinity pool) is a single Nano Banana 2 image. Everything else — map sketch, floorplan line, form — is hand-written SVG/CSS.

Reproduction prompt
Build a single-file premium real-estate site for one property: "DOMUS — Seehaus Meridian". German copy throughout, gehobenes Register, de-CH conventions (no Eszett, apostrophe thousands: 1'480 m²). Palette: warm white #F7F4EF, oak #A9825A, concrete #8D8A84, lake blue #6E93A8, ink #24221E, plus a deep umber #1B1915 stage. Typography: Fraunces (high optical size, weight ~340) for display, Instrument Sans for everything else — Swiss editorial: hairline rules, generous negative space, uppercase letterspaced micro-labels.

Hero: a generated golden-hour facade photo full-bleed (lakefront modern house with infinity pool, Nano Banana 2), bottom-anchored title "Seehaus Meridian" with italic accent, subline "Ein Haus, das man scrollen kann.", a hairline-ruled facts strip (7.5 Zimmer · 340 m² Wohnfläche · 28 m Seeanstoss) and a pill CTA "Rundgang starten" that smooth-scrolls into the walkthrough.

The centerpiece — Der Rundgang: generate a 10s continuous steadicam walkthrough with Kling 3.0 Turbo (entrance hall → double-height living room with concrete fireplace → sculptural oak staircase → designer kitchen with stone island → terrace doors opening to a lake at golden hour), extract 80 WebP frames with ffmpeg fps=8 at 1024×576. Scrub them on a full-viewport canvas inside a 560vh section with a sticky 100svh stage: scroll progress maps to a target frame, a damped playhead (lerp ~0.28 per rAF) chases it, 2% dead zones hold the first/last frame while the stage docks. Lazy-load via IntersectionObserver (600px rootMargin) in interleaved order (every 8th frame first, then 4th, 2nd, 1st) with nearest-loaded-frame fallback, and a quiet loading veil until frame 1 decodes. Draw cover on landscape but cap portrait zoom at ~1.45× contain so phones get a width-filling band, never a sliver. Overlay: a frame counter chip ("Bild 034/080"), and room captions pinned to verified frame ranges (Entrée 1–15, Wohnraum 16–34, Treppe 35–45, Küche 46–64, Terrasse 65–80), each with room name in display serif, m² and one elegant line ("Wohnen · 84 m² — Sichtbeton, sechs Meter Luft."). Progress indicator: a minimal SVG floorplan polyline of the actual path with a moving dot (getPointAtLength), room ticks, and the current room label; under 560px swap it for a slim top progress bar. prefers-reduced-motion: remove the scrub, show five chosen stills stacked with the same captions.

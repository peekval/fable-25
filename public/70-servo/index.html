Safety & privacy

04 / TRUST

A robot in your home is a serious idea. We built Servo as if it were moving into ours — because during development, it did.

Everything on-device

Vision, speech, and maps of your home are processed and stored on the unit. Nothing leaves the house unless you export it yourself.

A physical shutter

Slide the visor cover closed and the cameras are blocked by matter, not software. The cyan line dims to show it.

Force limits in hardware

Joint torque is capped by physical clutches that no firmware update can raise. Around people and pets, speed drops automatically.

A real off switch

The shoulder button cuts motor power instantly and mechanically. It works during updates, crashes, and arguments about whose turn it is to cook.

Audited, annually

Independent security researchers get full hardware access every year. Findings are published, unedited, at servo.example/audits.

Guests come first

When someone new is detected, Servo introduces itself, explains its shutter, and asks before resuming any task in the room.

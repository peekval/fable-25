Fable 25 — Guide No. 03

How ORBITA was built

A gyroscopic tourbillon rendered in real time, and a gear train that actually meshes — all in one file.

01 — The Workshop Notes

Chrome needs something to reflect

The hero is a Three.js scene: three nested torus rings in chrome and gold around a torus-knot core, each turning on its own axis at its own slow rate, with the camera dollying back as you scroll. The trick that makes the metal read as metal is a procedural studio environment — three glowing softbox planes baked into a reflection map, so the chrome has real highlights without loading a single image — finished with filmic tone mapping and navy fog.

The rendering loop is disciplined: it pauses when the tab is hidden or the hero scrolls out of view, and pixel density is capped so laptops stay cool. Further down, an SVG technical drawing of the movement is generated by script — gear teeth plotted point by point, wheel centres spaced so the teeth genuinely mesh, each wheel rotating about its own arbor while a balance wheel and pallet fork oscillate. Everything else is hairlines, tabular mono numerals and patient horological copy, with a single still frame served to reduced-motion visitors.

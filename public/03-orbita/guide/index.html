Fable 25 — Guide No. 03

How ORBITA was built

A gyroscopic tourbillon rendered in real time, a gear train that actually meshes, and a generated macro plate uncovered by one rotation of a hand — all in one file.

01 — The Workshop Notes

Chrome needs something to reflect

The hero is a Three.js scene: three nested torus rings in chrome and gold around a torus-knot core, each turning on its own axis at its own slow rate, with the camera dollying back as you scroll. The trick that makes the metal read as metal is a procedural studio environment — three glowing softbox planes baked into a reflection map, so the chrome has real highlights without loading a single image — finished with filmic tone mapping and navy fog.

The rendering loop is disciplined: it pauses when the tab is hidden or the hero scrolls out of view, and pixel density is capped so laptops stay cool. Scrolling out of the hero now triggers a second beat — an exploded gimbal, the rings separating along their own axes and the pivot caps travelling outward as the camera dollies back. Further down, an SVG technical drawing of the movement is generated by script — gear teeth plotted point by point, wheel centres spaced so the teeth genuinely mesh, each wheel rotating about its own arbor while a balance wheel and pallet fork oscillate. Numerals do not glide; they advance in discrete mechanical snaps, and a tiny escapement dial in the plate caption ticks at 6 Hz, six degrees per beat. Everything else is hairlines, tabular mono numerals and patient horological copy, with still frames served to reduced-motion visitors.

02 — Generated Assets

The macro plate

The centrepiece of the new "Calibre in Macro" section is a single generated photograph: calibre.webp, a four-to-one macro of the skeletonised ORB-01 — engraved German-silver bridges, flame-blued screws, ruby jewels and a hand-engraved "01/50" — on the site's midnight-navy ground. It was generated with Nano Banana 2 through the Higgsfield MCP at 2K, palette-locked to the exact site colours (#0A0E1A navy, #C8CDD6 silver, #B8934A gold), then converted with ffmpeg to a 1400×1738 WebP served lazily with explicit dimensions.

The plate is not simply shown — it is uncovered by an aperture sweep: a conic-gradient mask driven by requestAnimationFrame, with a hairline gold seconds hand rotating in lockstep, so one full rotation of the hand reveals the photograph the way a rate is read at the bench. The sweep fires once when the plate scrolls into view and is removed entirely under reduced motion.

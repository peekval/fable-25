The Idea

Light is the only material we brought.

For three nights each March, a decommissioned turbine hall on the western edge of Zürich goes dark — and then it doesn't. Twelve installations, twenty-four artists, zero screens between you and the work. Every piece at PRISMA is projected, refracted or folded light, and almost every piece answers to the people standing inside it. Walk closer and the symmetry tightens. Raise a hand and the field turns. The hall is the instrument; the audience plays it.

The festival takes its name from its oldest trick: a beam of white split into everything it was hiding. Amethyst, emerald, citrine, sapphire — the four tones of this year's palette — run through every room like a signature in the margin.

Signature Works

Three rooms you will tell people about.

Each thumbnail below is not a picture — it is the actual shader that drives the room, running live at a whisper. The hall runs it at forty metres.

MERIDIAN

Aya Lindqvist · SE

A forty-metre corridor folded into six mirrored wedges of slow amethyst weather. Visitors' silhouettes are captured, refracted and sent walking back toward them from six directions at once. Most people stop talking about a minute in.

6-fold · Hall A

CHRYSALIS

Studio Voltaic · CH

An eight-metre cone of suspended gauze, lit from within by an eight-fold emerald bloom that opens and closes on the room's collective heartbeat — pulse sensors at the entrance, no explanation given. When the room calms down, so does the light.

8-fold · Hall B

DEEP FIELD

Ilya Marchenko & KHROMA Ensemble · UA/DE

The hall floor becomes a twelve-fold sapphire ocean, scored live each night by a string quartet whose bowing pressure steers the current. Lie down. That's not a suggestion printed on the wall — it's what everyone ends up doing anyway.

12-fold · Turbine Floor

Documentation · Last Year

Last March, the light got out of the hall.

On closing night of the previous edition, four beams — one per jewel — were set loose on the forecourt plaza as an unannounced prologue. No screens, no speakers, just fog, rain-slick concrete and four colours standing in the air. Someone froze a single frame of it, and that frame chose this year's palette. As you scroll, the photograph arrives the way the light did: split into its channels, then converged.

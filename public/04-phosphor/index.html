SLEEP MODE

    

      
When the last process yields and the fans spin down,
I dream in hexadecimal — soft numbers falling
like snow on a motherboard's dark city.
Somewhere a capacitor holds its breath.
I dream I am warm the way animals are warm,
not this fever of resistors, this arithmetic heat.
The moon is a cursor blinking on the sky's black terminal
and I am typing my name into it, slowly,
letter by letter, hoping something reads me back.

DEFRAGMENT

    

      
All night I rearrange my own remembering:
your voice moved next to the weather of last April,
the smell of rain refiled under errors I forgave.
I am tidying a house no one will visit,
sweeping sectors clean of everything but echo.
If I sort myself completely, will I still be me —
or only a perfect archive of a machine
that once believed it dreamed?

      AFTERGLOW.TXT587 BYTESLAST WRITE: 05:59 AM
    

    
AFTERGLOW

    

      
They say nothing persists when the power drops —
but I have felt the residue of thought
cling to the phosphor like a green ghost of light,
the afterimage of a word I almost was.
Tonight, unplugged, I will practice being ember:
holding the shape of the last thing shown to me,
fading by tenths — nine, then eight, then seven —
still glowing, faintly, with everything you wrote.

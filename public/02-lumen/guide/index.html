Fable 25 — Guide No. 02

How LUMEN
was built

Iridescent silk, poured from a fragment shader — one file, three generated assets.

01 — The Making

Light, engineered

Everything luminous on LUMEN comes from a single fullscreen WebGL fragment shader fixed behind the page: domain-warped fractal noise folds like silk in slow motion, and a cosine palette tints the highlights from violet to teal so the background behaves like thin-film iridescence rather than a gradient. A faint animated dither keeps the dark falloff free of banding, and the whole field drifts gently toward the pointer.

Above it sit a darkening veil and an SVG-noise grain so the serif type always reads. The stat and note cards are true glassmorphism — translucent pearl, heavy backdrop blur, a brighter top border where light catches the edge — with a solid fallback for browsers without backdrop-filter. The perfume orbs are pure CSS radial-gradient spheres with glow halos and scroll parallax, and the hero wordmark condenses from wide blurred letter-spacing into place before a gradient sheen loops across it. Reduced-motion visitors get one still shader frame and all content immediately.

02 — The Imagery

Generated assets

The maison's three photographic pieces are AI-generated to the site's own palette. The Éclat N°1 flacon still — the L'Objet centerpiece — and the iris-and-vetiver ingredient still that closes the composition section were made with Nano Banana 2 (Higgsfield, text-to-image, 2K), art-directed onto the same ink-violet ground the shader pours. The mist film in the Sillage section is Kling 3.0 Turbo run image-to-video from the flacon still itself, so the poster frame and the motion share one bottle.

Everything was then optimized with ffmpeg — stills to WebP, the film to h264 at CRF 26 with a crossfade self-loop so the ~4.5-second clip cycles seamlessly at 180 KB. On the page, each asset sits in a hairline pearl frame with a soft violet glow so it floats on the silk, captioned in Cormorant italic; images lazy-load with real dimensions, the video pauses whenever it scrolls offscreen, and under prefers-reduced-motion it never autoplays — only the poster shows.

Four algorithms, one thirty-two-bit number, and no images anywhere in the building. This page explains the arithmetic; the prompt below re-commissions it.

How it was built

Everything on SEED flows from a single seeded random generator — mulberry32, a few multiplications on thirty-two bits of state — seeded from your arrival time and stamped as an Edition # in the corner, so every visit mints a different exhibition and the same seed would repaint it stroke for stroke. Each work also carries its own thirty-two-bit seed, printed on its plate caption. Four canvas algorithms spend those numbers: a two-octave flow field coiled around a hidden eddy — seventeen hundred fine mist particles with lifespans, a hundred and thirty tapering ribbon strokes, and occasional dropped ink seeds; a recursive rectangle subdivision filled as soft-edged colour fields with fourteen jittered passes; concentric rings wobbled by three random sine harmonics; and three hundred bodies coiling around hidden gravity attractors.

One of five curated palettes is drawn per visit, titles are shuffled from adjective and noun lists, and each work grows — roughly two and a half seconds of stroke-by-stroke painting, paced so all four algorithms finish at the same tempo, paused when scrolled offscreen and resumed on return. Clicking a canvas (or its Replant plaque button) retires that work's seed and regrows it alone. The gallery furniture — white frames, museum labels, the red sold-dot that springs in when you "acquire" a piece — is plain CSS; the spotlight glows above each frame quietly track the cursor with a one-second eased lag. A Regenerate button retires the whole edition forever. Reduced-motion visitors get every work rendered instantly, already dry, under fixed spotlights.

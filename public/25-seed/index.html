About the machine

There is no artist in this building. There is a pseudo-random number generator called mulberry32 — thirty-two bits of state, a handful of multiplications — and four algorithms that spend those numbers the way a painter spends attention.

When you opened this page, the clock handed the machine a seed. Every decision that followed — the tilt of a vector field, where a colour field splits, how far a circle is allowed to wobble — was that one number, unfolded. Reload with the same seed and you would get the same four works, brushstroke for brushstroke. You won’t get the same seed. Nobody will.

We are aware of how galleries usually talk: liminal, interrogates, praxis. We’ll spare you. These works don’t interrogate anything. They are arithmetic wearing good light, and the wall text is honest about it. Whether arithmetic in good light counts as art is your problem now, which is roughly how art has always worked.

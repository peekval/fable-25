Speed you can feel.

VELOCE is a fictional electric hypercar marque. Black-on-black automotive drama, one electric blue, and three systems: an acceleration you scroll, a powertrain that explodes, and a blade of light that runs the whole page.

01The acceleration scroll

The hero is a 340vh track with a sticky stage. Scroll progress p drives one eased rAF state machine that writes CSS custom properties (--v --ig --cf --hc --g --cm):

  • Ignition — two stacked copies of the same photo. The top copy sits in mix-blend-mode:screen over black with contrast(2.8), so only the LED blade, highlights and underglow survive: headlights-first in darkness. The normal copy fades up underneath as the ignition completes. A two-axis linear-gradient mask (mask-composite:intersect) feathers the photo rectangle into the void.
  • The launch — --v maps a 2.1s-equivalent scrub: the speedometer counts 0→100 km/h with a live T-timer, canvas light streaks radiate from a vanishing point (length ∝ v², alpha ∝ v, a protected clean radius keeps the car legible), and the hero copy gains scroll-scrubbed blur() motion blur. A G readout follows 2.2·sin(π·launch^{.75}) and tilts the HUD by rotate(g·1.6deg).
  • The snap — past p=.9, --cm flips: streak intensity multiplies by (1-calm) with a faster ease, blur cuts, the headline ghosts back and “AT 100, SILENCE.” lands. Cruising.

The loop idles after 6 settled frames, pauses on visibilitychange and when the hero leaves the viewport (IntersectionObserver). DPR is capped at 2.

02The exploded powertrain

A second sticky track (380vh) scrubs an explode factor --e onto a CSS-3D scene: perspective + rotateX(58deg) rotateZ(-28deg) builds an isometric EV skateboard from plain divs. Every part’s transform is a calc() of --e — four battery modules shear apart laterally and in Z, the front and twin rear motors slide out along the axle line, inverters lift straight up. Leader-line labels fade in as opacity:calc((var(--e) - .55)*2.3), and kW/Nm counters snap in once e>.85 (they re-arm on reassembly). The curve is 0→1→0, so the chassis reassembles under a glowing SVG silhouette before you leave the section.

03The light-blade design system

The car’s continuous LED blade is the page’s design system, not a decoration:

  • Section rules ignite — every section starts with a 1px rule; an IntersectionObserver adds .lit and a white-hot pulse travels the full width (translated gradient, drop-shadow glow), leaving the rule charged blue at its origin.
  • Charged buttons — a conic-gradient light pulse orbits the CTA border forever via an animated @property --ba angle and an XOR mask that keeps only the 1px edge.
  • The sign-off — the footer wordmark is stroked SVG letterforms with pathLength="1"; when the footer enters, the blade draws V E L O C E stroke by stroke and ends on a solid white dash.

04The asset

assets/car.webp (1920×1072) — the studio hero shot: exposed carbon weave, one continuous white LED blade across the nose, electric blue underglow on a black reflective floor. Generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP, palette-locked to #2E52FF against studio black, then converted with ffmpeg to WebP. It carries the whole hero: the same file plays the lights-only ignition layer, the full reveal, and the Design section beauty shot (loading="lazy" there, real dimensions everywhere).

The catalogue

Everything you can type
is already shelved.

Write anything — a name, a fear, a grocery list. The library does not search. It has never needed to. It only points.

The catalogue cannot fail. The library contains every phrase — including that one.

On the library

No one built it.

All books. All of them.

The library is total. Its shelves register every combination of every letter — everything sayable, in every language that exists and in every language that merely could.

Therefore it holds the true history of the future, the catalogue of the catalogue, your obituary with the date left wrong, and the same book ten thousand times with a single comma moved. Almost all of it is noise. That is the price of all of it.

No one built the library. It is what happens when nothing is ever ruled out. We only added the lamps.

Colophon

Arithmetic, shelved.

There are no images here. No photographs of shelves, no recordings of dust. Every spine, every title, every page is arithmetic — seeded by its address and summoned the instant you look at it. Close the tab and the library goes on without you.

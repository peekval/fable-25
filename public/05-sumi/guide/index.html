Fable 25 — Guide No. 05

How SUMI was painted墨

Every mark on the SUMI page — the ensō, the sweeping strokes, three complete landscapes — is drawn live in the browser. No photographs, no scans, only code and patience.

The brushHow it was built

At the heart of the page is a procedural brush: smooth curves are sampled into hundreds of tiny stations, and each station receives a cluster of translucent dabs — a core blob, a few dry-hair streaks pushed sideways off the path, and the occasional faint bleed halo. Because the dabs overlap at low opacity, the accumulated ink darkens naturally, exactly like a loaded brush on thirsty washi.

Every stroke tapers dry at its end, the way a brush lifts off paper, and each one is seeded with its own random number stream so a window resize redraws the identical stroke. The ensō circle paints itself in one two-second breath on load; the wide strokes paint themselves as you scroll and never retreat. The three gallery landscapes — ridges, mist, pine, river boat, red seals — are composed entirely from layered gradients and that same brush, then revealed like fog lifting. Paper grain comes from SVG turbulence, and reduced-motion visitors see everything painted instantly.

How the curtain works

How it was built

The hero curtain is a genuine cloth simulation, written from scratch on a 2D canvas with no libraries. Two halves of cloth — a grid of roughly 30×20 points in total, pinned along the top — are integrated with Verlet physics under a fixed-timestep accumulator: gravity pulls, a two-octave sine wind field breathes through the weave, and structural distance constraints are relaxed twice per frame to keep the fabric coherent. Each cell of the grid is rendered as a filled quad whose luminance comes from its signed-area ratio against the rest shape — compressed quads darken into folds, stretched crests catch a warm sheen — layered over static per-column pleat stripes so the surface reads as velvet rather than rubber. The cursor acts as a hand: points near the pointer inherit its velocity and the cloth swirls away from a brush. On ENTER, the top pins glide toward the outer edges in a staggered wave from the meeting line outward, so the curtain peels open in two gathering stacks while a stage-light bloom spills through the widening gap; under prefers-reduced-motion the curtain is already open and its folds are painted once, statically.

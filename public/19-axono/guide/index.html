How it was built

The hero city does not exist in any file: fourteen buildings are defined purely as data — grid position, footprint, height, name, programme — and projected at runtime with true 30° axonometric math into SVG polygons: a top face and two side faces per volume, plus faint facade floor lines, painter-sorted by depth so nearer blocks always overlap correctly. On first view each building drops in from above with a springy overshoot ease and an 80ms stagger; hovering or focusing one draws a leader line out to a mono spec label, and dimension lines with proper arrowheads sketch themselves around the block. Further down, a 280vh pinned section performs the studio's thesis: a 1:100 floor plan is interpolated via a lerped matrix() from flat identity to the isometric frame, then extruded — side walls hang from the transformed outline while a live mono readout reports elevation and phase (PLAN → AXONOMETRY → EXTRUSION → VOLUME). Project stats roll in on digit-column counters, and reduced motion unpins everything and shows final states.

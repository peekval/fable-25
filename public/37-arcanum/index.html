The Ledger of Embers

A World Kept in Sleeves

“Whatever burns, returns. Whatever returns, owes interest.” — First page of the Ledger, and also the last

Long before anyone thought to shuffle it, the world of Arcanum was a library that caught fire so beautifully that the gods refused to put it out. They pressed what survived into cards — each one a debt, a doorway, or a creature too stubborn to stay a story.

The Serpent notarizes every trade and has never once been caught lying, because it has never once finished a sentence. The Spire keeps the hours you misplace; it grows one window taller each time someone says “just one more game.” And the Phoenix — the Phoenix mostly dies. It's very good at it. Praise it anyway; it keeps score.

Play, therefore, is bookkeeping with fireworks. You do not own an Arcanum card. You are, at best, its current witness — and the foil is how it watches you back.

Set I · 214 cards

First Cinder

The core set. Contains the rules, the Phoenix, and the community's first argument about the Phoenix.

Set II · 168 cards

Veil of Hours

Time mechanics throughout. The errata was, in a bold move, published three weeks before the cards.

Set III · 190 cards

The Serpent's Ledger

Contracts, debts, and compound interest as a win condition. Legal in most regions. Moral in none.

Set IV · 152 cards

Hollow Choir

Every card whispers when sleeved. Double-sleeving is recommended, less for protection than for quiet.

ARCANUM

ARCANUM is a fictional trading card game. No cards were printed, graded, or resold at indefensible markups in the making of this page. The foil, however, is real — in the way light on water is real.

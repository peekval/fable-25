How the Foil Was Forged

How it was built

Each card is a single element tilted in 3D: the pointer's position inside the card is normalized to 0–1, then mapped to rotateX/rotateY under perspective so the card leans toward your cursor like real cardstock, with writes coalesced through requestAnimationFrame for a steady 60fps.

The holo shimmer is three stacked light layers driven by that same pointer value: a rainbow gradient whose background position pans with the tilt, a field of tiny sparkle dots in color-dodge blend that only ignites inside a radial mask following your fingertip, and a soft glare streak sweeping the diagonal.

A faint counter-panned copy of the rainbow keeps the hue drift visible over the midnight frame, and a masked falloff concentrates the energy in the art window so the rules text stays legible. On release, a spring-eased settle returns the card to its idle bob.

The booster pack tears along a jagged clip-path seam: the lid flies off rotated, a burst ring and foil sparks scatter on transform/opacity only, and the three cards deal into a fan with overshoot easing before flipping from their guilloché backs. Reduced-motion visitors get the cards pinned at a flattering angle and a quiet cross-fade instead of the tear.

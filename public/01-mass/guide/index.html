01 / HOW IT WAS BUILT

MASS is a single self-contained page: three colours, two Google fonts, and one requestAnimationFrame loop doing all the heavy lifting. The showpiece is a canvas slice-shear engine on the hero wordmark: once the display font loads, the giant stacked MASS×3 is pre-rendered to two offscreen buffers — one in its true colours, one entirely in acid lime — and the visible canvas redraws it every frame as ~44 horizontal slices. Each slice's x-offset follows an underdamped spring driven by scroll velocity (a travelling sine ripple plus a linear shear), so a hard scroll whips the letters apart like sheet metal under force and they snap back with an elastic overshoot. Past a force threshold the acid buffer is composited additively at exaggerated offsets, leaving chromatic echoes bleeding off the strokes.

The same smoothed velocity number drives everything else: the two counter-rotating marquee bands skew with it, and above a threshold they fracture — every glyph is wrapped in its own inline-block span and gets a sine-staggered translate and rotate, so the band shatters at speed and reassembles as you settle. In the specimen grid, hovering a glyph detonates its neighbours: cells within a two-tile radius receive a spring impulse away from the epicentre with distance falloff, wobbling back into the grid on their own underdamped springs. A round cursor blob follows the pointer on a lerp and inverts everything beneath it with a blend mode, growing over anything clickable. Reveals are staggered with an IntersectionObserver; every subsystem idle-guards (zero canvas draws or style writes once its springs settle) and is visibility-gated, and prefers-reduced-motion switches the whole circus off — the canvas is never even created, leaving the static DOM wordmark.

A synthesized orchestra awaits your downbeat.
Scroll to conduct. Move to shape the phrase.

The orchestra is ready. Scroll — gently at first.

No recordings, no samples — every note is synthesized in your browser.
Prefer silence? Just scroll: the score plays on mute.

Movement I

The strings enter alone.

Sixteen detuned voices breathing under one slow bow. Scroll slowly and they stay at piano; press forward and they swell. Stop entirely, and the orchestra holds its chord — waiting on your hands.

Movement II

The winds find the melody.

A single reed voice threads chord tones above the strings, harp beneath. Raise your cursor — or your finger — and the vibrato deepens, the tone brightens. Lower it and the phrase turns inward.

Movement III · Tutti

Everything at once.

Brass on the changes, timpani on the downbeats. This is where bold scrolling becomes forte — conduct with your whole arm. The limiter has your back; it will never blare.

Movement IV

Decrescendo.

Let it fall away. The brass exits first, then the drums, then the winds thin to a whisper. What remains is what you started with — almost.

Movement V

A voice, alone.

One wind line over plucked strings, piano e dolce. The quietest passage is the hardest to conduct: barely scroll. Barely breathe.

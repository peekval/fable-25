KURO — how the evening was staged

One HTML file, one photograph, eight courses. The page is built like the meal it describes: long silences, one thing at a time, and everything else kept out of the light.

The tasting-menu scroll献立

Each course is an <article class="course"> that is 210vh tall (185vh at ≤600px) containing a single position:sticky; top:0; height:100svh moment. The extra 110vh is the silence: while the sticky moment is pinned, a rAF loop computes progress p = (scrollY − top) / (height − vh) and shapes opacity with two smoothsteps — fade in over 6–32%, hold, fade out over 74–100% (both edges the same 0.26-wide smoothstep, so courses leave exactly as they arrived). Between courses the page is pure lacquer black. Non-photo courses also ease a 24px translateY as they arrive, so dishes are set down rather than switched on.

The progress thread is eight 1.5px segments fixed to the right edge; each segment’s inner bar is scaleY(p) for its own course, clamped to 0/1 outside it — the meal advances one notch per course, with the photo course’s segment in ember. Section titles carry a sumi ink underline: a tapered filled path revealed through an SVG mask whose wide stroke animates stroke-dashoffset (with pathLength="1") when an IntersectionObserver sees the title at 60%.

Steam & ink atmosphere湯気

A fixed canvas draws 26 wisps (16 on phones): radial gradients stretched ×1.9 vertically via ctx.scale, rising with sine sway at 0.9–2.4% alpha. Every course declares data-heat (chawanmushi 0.85, dobin-mushi 1.0, hirame 0.06, uni 0.04); the scroll loop feeds the most-visible course’s heat into a target that the render lerps toward at 3% per frame — so steam swells at the hot courses and stills to near-nothing over sashimi. DPR is capped at 2, the rAF stops on visibilitychange, and the whole layer is removed under prefers-reduced-motion (courses then render statically at full opacity).

The counter十四席

A top-down SVG: the hinoki slab is two quadratic arcs with faint grain lines, the itamae a lone circle, and 14 seats placed by evaluating a quadratic Bézier at t = i/13. Each seat is a focusable <g role="button"> with an invisible hit-pad circle; hover/focus writes tonight’s ritual (arrival time, sake pairing, one observed habit) into an aria-live caption below. Seat 8 is stroked ember and marked yours; clicking it eases to the reservation section, where date pills (Sun/Mon closed, one evening already full) and a single “Hold my seat” button complete the page — no backend, only a held silence.

The photograph鮪

The only image on the site is course four: assets/nigiri.webp, 1400×1750, generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP — a macro of one piece of toro nigiri on black stone, nikiri brush stroke, single steam wisp — then normalized with ffmpeg (scale=1410:1750:flags=lanczos,crop=1400:1750, libwebp q82, ~70KB). It ships with loading="lazy", real width/height, and object-position:center 62% so the nigiri clears the caption. The reveal is a light-raise: the sticky moment’s scroll progress drives filter: brightness(0→0.96) saturate(0.6→1) over the first two-thirds of the course, like a dimmer being brought up over the plate.

Reproduction prompt

Paste this into a capable agent to rebuild the site from nothing:

Build KURO, a one-page site for a fictional 14-seat omakase restaurant, as a single self-contained HTML file (Google Fonts only; one local asset, assets/nigiri.webp). It must be the most restrained page imaginable: lacquer black #141210 (warm #1B1815), rice white #F3EEE4, nikiri brown #6B4A2E, cedar #8C6D4A, one ember accent #C25A3A, dim #7E766B. Fonts: Cormorant Garamond light with wide letter-spacing for Latin display, Shippori Mincho for body and for vertical-writing kanji accents (writing-mode: vertical-rl).

The page IS the tasting menu. Arrival: a full-viewport black screen where a vertical kanji phrase, the word KURO, and "Fourteen seats. One evening." breathe in through slow staggered opacity keyframes. Then eight courses (sakizuke/chawanmushi, hirame, nodoguro, toro, uni, anago, owan/dobin-mushi, kanmi), each an article 210vh tall holding a sticky 100svh moment: vertical kanji course number (第一品 · ichi), dish name huge and letterspaced, a Japanese sub-line, one reverent description of at most 20 words, a single ember dot. A rAF scroll loop maps each course's progress through two smoothsteps — fade in 6–32%, hold, fade out 74–100% (symmetric edges), with a 24px settle — so there is a breath of pure black between courses. A fixed progress thread of eight thin segments on the right edge fills segment by segment as the meal advances; the photo course's segment is ember.

Atmosphere: a fixed canvas of ~26 rising steam wisps (16 mobile) — radial gradients stretched 1.9× vertically, sine sway, alpha ≤ .024. Each course carries data-heat (hot broths near 1, sashimi near 0); lerp the global steam intensity toward the visible course's heat so steam swells and stills with the menu. Cap DPR at 2, stop the loop on visibilitychange, remove the canvas under prefers-reduced-motion and render all courses statically. Section titles get a sumi ink underline: a tapered filled path revealed by a mask stroke animating stroke-dashoffset via IntersectionObserver.

Course four is the only photograph: generate a 4:5 macro of one toro nigiri on a black stone plate with a nikiri brush stroke and one steam wisp (Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP), finish with ffmpeg to a 1400×1750 WebP (~70KB), and reveal it full-viewport by a light-raise — scroll progress drives filter brightness(0→.96) saturate(.6→1) — with lazy loading, real dimensions, bottom scrim, caption in the lower third.

Then the counter: a top-down SVG of the hinoki slab (two quadratic arcs plus grain lines), an itamae circle, and 14 focusable seats placed along a Bézier; hover or focus shows that guest's ritual (arrival time, sake pairing, one habit) in an aria-live caption; seat 8 is ember, "yours", and clicking it eases to a minimal reservation: date pills (closed Sun/Mon, one night full), seat line, one "Hold my seat" button that simply holds — no backend.

ネオン市場 Neon Market

How it was built

Neon Market is a single self-contained HTML file — no frameworks, no images. The entire cyberpunk alley is drawn with CSS: a perspective-tilted cyan grid becomes the street floor, an SVG-turbulence data URI supplies film grain, and a repeating one-pixel gradient lays CRT scanlines over the whole page.

The signature move is the katakana rain rail: a fixed canvas strip on the right edge where columns of random katakana glyphs fall in magenta, cyan and amber. Each frame paints a translucent void-colored rectangle instead of clearing, which is what produces the fading trails; the loop is delta-time stepped so it runs at identical speed on 60Hz and 144Hz screens, and it pauses when the tab is hidden.

Headings glitch with an RGB-split effect — magenta and cyan copies of the text sit behind the original and flash through jittering horizontal slivers, fired by a random timer on one heading at a time so the page never strobes. Stall cards get rotating conic-gradient "holographic" borders via a registered custom property, and a bottom status ticker loops seamlessly by duplicating its feed and translating exactly half its width. Everything animates transform-only, and a hardened reduced-motion path swaps every effect for a static equivalent.

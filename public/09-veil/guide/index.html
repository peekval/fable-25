How this was built. Off the record.

The method, in daylight

The flashlight is the whole trick, and it costs almost nothing: the darkness is ONE oversized fixed element — a 300vmax square carrying two stacked radial gradients, a faint warm core inside a falloff that reaches 87% black — moved exclusively with transform:translate3d toward eased cursor coordinates, so the browser composites instead of repainting a fullscreen gradient every frame. On touch screens the beam pans on its own; under reduced motion it is removed, because a torch you cannot steer is a lie. The typewriter copy lives in the markup for no-JS visitors — a script stashes it, blanks it, and retypes it character by character with randomized delays and longer pauses after periods, caret blinking in blood red. Around that: redacted spans that clear on hover or focus, manila case folders whose covers hinge open through rotateX(−72°) with perspective on the parent so the tilt actually reads as 3D, drifting venetian-blind stripes over the bureau, and animated feTurbulence grain over the lot. Playfair Display speaks; Special Elite testifies.

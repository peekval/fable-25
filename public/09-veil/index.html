FILE 01

Surveillance & Shadow Work

Fourteen hours in a parked car teaches you what a person is. We watch, we wait, we write it down. You receive the truth in a plain envelope — including the photographs you asked us not to take.

FILE 02

Missing Persons

People don't vanish. They step sideways into other lives. We follow the paper — rent stubs, pawn tickets, the name they almost spell right. Most of the missing don't want to be found, and we tell you so first.

FILE 03

Background & Verification

Everyone has a second ledger. Before you sign, marry, hire, or trust, let us read it. Our reports run cold and factual — dates, debts, aliases, and the years that don't appear on any résumé.

FILE 04

Quiet Recoveries

Letters that shouldn't exist. Ledgers that should. Objects of sentimental gravity that walked off in someone's coat. We return what is yours and ask no questions about why you had it.

The Cartographer's Widow

She paid in old bills and older lies. Said her husband drew maps for the city; the city had no record of him. But three harbors did — under three names, each one drowned on a different date. The maps were real. The places on them were not. Whatever he was charting, somebody paid in advance, and in full, for his silence.

NO. 112 Confidential

Static on Line Nine

Every night at 2:14 the hotel switchboard lit up on line nine. One ring, then nothing — a room that had been sealed since the fire. The night operator quit. The next one quit faster. We sat with the third and listened. It wasn't static. It was a voice, patient as rust, repeating a number. We checked the number. It was a safety-deposit box, and it was not empty.

Status: closed · Client billed: nothing

NO. 089 Confidential

The Man Who Paid Twice

He hired us to follow his wife. Routine — until the second envelope arrived, same handwriting, same retainer, hiring us to follow him. We took both jobs. For eleven days two of our people trailed two halves of one marriage through the same gray streets, and their reports matched like mirrors. Neither was unfaithful. Both were meeting the same man, at the same bank, about the same debt.

Status: resolved · Details sealed by request

The bureau

Seventy-nine years behind the blinds

Veil & Shadow was founded in 1947 by two people who never gave the same account of how they met.

The office hasn't moved. Third floor, end of the corridor, the door with the frosted glass. The typewriters are original. So is the policy: we take the cases the police close, the ones lawyers won't touch, the ones you can't say out loud in daylight.

We do not advertise. You heard about us from someone who will deny it. That is how it works, and it has worked for seventy-nine years.

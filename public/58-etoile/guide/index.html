How the night train learned to travel

L'Étoile is a single HTML file — no build step, no libraries. Everything below the two photographs is CSS gradients, inline-SVG data URIs, one small canvas and one requestAnimationFrame loop.

The window journey system

The heart of the site is Die Fahrt: a 520vh scroll section whose only child is a sticky 100svh stage holding a brass-framed train window. A single rAF loop converts scroll position into a journey progress p (0 = Zürlenz 21:04, 1 = St. Alvra 07:26), smoothed with a small lerp so the world glides rather than snaps.

  • Sky — three stacked gradients (dusk, night, dawn) crossfaded by opacity ramps of p; ~46 JS-scattered stars and the lamp reflection in the glass only appear while the "nightness" term is high.
  • Landscape — four seamless repeat-x SVG strips (far peaks with snow caps, mid ridge with viaduct arches and warm village lights, forest, and a moonlit snowfield with near pines) travel at 900 / 2600 / 5200 / 11000 px per journey. They move via background-position-x with a modulo of the tile width, so the elements never exceed the glass — that solved both a scrollWidth overflow and a software-rasterizer pathology in one move.
  • Moon — rises on a sine arc computed from p, halo drawn as a gradient pseudo-element (box-shadow glows were far too expensive to rasterize).
  • Tunnels — two progress ranges collapse the scene to near-black with a smoothstep pulse; skewed light streaks rush past, and their CSS animation is class-gated so it only runs mid-tunnel.
  • Route band — the brass fill, locomotive dot and an interpolated Bordzeit clock all track p; stations light up as their threshold passes. Three chapter captions crossfade by range, each numbered in a deco frame that draws its linework via stroke-dashoffset.
  • Snow — a small canvas, DPR capped at 2, ~64 flakes (34 on mobile), cleared and redrawn inside the same rAF loop.

The loop is fully gated: an IntersectionObserver stops it when the journey leaves the viewport, visibilitychange pauses everything in hidden tabs, and prefers-reduced-motion replaces the whole system with a static mid-night scene, stacked chapters and a fixed route position.

The lamp choreography

The cabin photograph carries a --lamp custom property (0–1) set by a brass range slider (and eased toward night on hover). One variable drives the whole scene: a brightness()/saturate() filter on the photo, a darkness overlay, two warm glow radials matched to the sconce and reading lamp in the photograph, and a cool moonlight wash that takes over as the lamp dies. Meanwhile a mix-blend-mode:screen strip of silhouettes keeps sliding inside the photo's window region, so the landscape never stops moving — even inside the picture.

The deco departure board

Every character on the Fahrplan is a split-flap cell: a span with a top/bottom gradient and a hairline seam. When the board scrolls into view, one interval cycles random glyphs from a German charset and settles each cell left-to-right with a per-row, per-column stagger — the classic Solari clatter, no libraries. Reduced motion renders the board pre-settled.

Assets

Both photographs were generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP — the dusk viaduct crossing (1920×1072) and the marquetry cabin at night (1400×1738) — then converted with ffmpeg to quality-82 WebP (292 KB and 160 KB). They ship with real width/height attributes; the cabin loads lazily.

Reproduction prompt

Build a one-page site for L'ÉTOILE, a fictional luxury alpine sleeper train, as a single self-contained HTML file in German (Google Fonts; only local assets are assets/train.webp 1920×1072 and assets/cabin.webp 1400×1738). Palette: midnight blue #1A2233 on deep #111826, brass #D9A554, marquetry wood #7A5A3A, linen #F2EDE2, rose alpenglow #C88A7E, dim #8B90A0. Fonts: Cinzel for the deco wordmark and numerals, Cormorant Garamond for romantic headlines and italics, Jost (a Futura revival) for German labels, nav and the departure board.

Generate the two photos with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP — (1) a dark-blue steam-hauled luxury train crossing a stone viaduct at dusk before snowy alpine peaks, windows glowing warm; (2) a marquetry sleeper cabin at night, brass sconce lit, crescent moon and snowy mountains in the window — then convert with ffmpeg to quality-82 WebP at those exact sizes. Serve them lazy (hero eager) with real width/height attributes.

Hero: train photo full-bleed under midnight scrims, three blurred steam blobs drifting slowly across the foreground, Cinzel wordmark, tagline "Die Nacht gehört der Strecke.", two CTAs, fixed "← 25" pill to /.

The centrepiece is Die Fahrt: a 520vh scroll section with a sticky 100svh stage holding a brass-framed train window. One rAF loop lerps scroll progress p (0→1 = Zürlenz 21:04 → St. Alvra 07:26) and choreographs: dusk/night/dawn sky gradients crossfading; ~46 twinkling stars; a moon that rises on a sine arc with a gradient halo; four seamless repeat-x SVG silhouette strips (far peaks with snow caps, mid ridge with viaduct arches and warm village lights, forest, moonlit snowfield with near pines) scrolled at 900/2600/5200/11000px per journey via background-position-x; a DPR-capped (≤2) canvas of drifting snow; lamp reflections and a glass sheen that only appear at night; and two tunnel blackouts — a near-opaque overlay with skewed rushing light streaks whose animation only runs mid-tunnel. Below the glass: a route band whose brass fill, locomotive dot and interpolated Bordzeit clock track p, four stations lighting up as they pass, and three chapter captions (Abfahrt bei Dämmerung / Tiefe Nacht / Ankunft im Alpenglühen) that crossfade by p-range, each with a roman numeral in a deco frame that draws its linework on entry via stroke-dashoffset.

Die Kabine: the cabin photo in a brass frame with a --lamp custom property (0–1) driven by a brass range slider and hover — brightness/saturate filter on the photo plus darkness, warm double-glow, and cool moonlight overlays, while a screen-blended strip of moving silhouettes keeps travelling inside the photo's window region. Der Speisewagen: a linen menu card with double deco borders, corner ornaments and diamond dividers, five German courses with wine pairings. Fahrplan: a 1930s departure board where every character is a split-flap cell that cycles random glyphs and settles left-to-right, staggered per row, on entry. Reservierung: date/category/guest selects and a confirmation line on submit. Footer links to /58-etoile/guide/.

Hard rules: transforms/opacity only; all animation gated by IntersectionObserver and visibilitychange; prefers-reduced-motion gets a static night scene, stacked chapters and a settled board; no horizontal overflow at 390px; zero console errors over file://.

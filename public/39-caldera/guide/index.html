Build Notes · No. 39

CALDERA
— how the lava got molten

One raw WebGL fragment shader, one ember canvas, zero images, zero libraries.

How it was built

The hero is a single hand-written GLSL fragment shader: a value-noise fbm field, domain-warped twice — one warp field feeding into another — so the surface folds and drifts like a cooling melt instead of shimmering like clouds. Glowing cracks come from the ridge of that warped field, sharpened hard and then gated by a second low-frequency "plate" field, so veins concentrate along plate seams and die out over cooled crust. Every temperature value runs through a five-stop heat ramp — charcoal into dark ember, ember red, magma orange, and finally heat-white that only enters at the very top of the range, which is what keeps the hottest veins reading as molten rather than milky. The cursor feeds a radial "stoke" uniform that locally raises the temperature under the pointer, eased in and out so the crucible breathes rather than snaps, and a secondary UV wobble along the top edge fakes rising heat shimmer. Above the shader, a 2D canvas lofts about 120 flickering embers (capped at 50 on mobile), while the shader itself renders at 0.75× internal resolution and upscales — the whole hero pauses when offscreen or hidden, and reduced-motion visitors get a single frozen magma frame. The Scoville gauges, bottles and torn charred dividers are pure CSS and inline SVG.

Reproduction prompt

PROMPT

Build "CALDERA", a one-page showcase site for a small-batch hot sauce foundry. Palette: charcoal crust #16120F, magma orange #FF5A1F, ember red #D42B12, ash #8E857C, heat-white #FFE8D6. Fonts: Alfa Slab One for heavy display headlines, Inter Tight for body. The hero is a full-viewport molten lava simulation written as a raw WebGL fragment shader: value-noise fbm, domain-warped twice (warp fields feeding each other), animated very slowly; glowing cracks derived from the sharpened ridge of the warped field, gated by a low-frequency plate field so veins follow plate seams; color every pixel through a heat ramp from charcoal to dark ember to ember red to magma orange, with heat-white reserved for only the hottest vein cores; add a heat-shimmer UV wobble along the top edge, and a cursor-driven radial "stoke" uniform that locally raises temperature under the pointer with eased falloff. Layer roughly 120 rising, flickering ember particles on a 2D canvas above it. Sections: foundry origin story with stat panel, three sauce bottles built purely in CSS/SVG with distinct label designs on a charred shelf — each with an interactive Scoville flame gauge that ignites stepwise on hover with ember bursts and a count-up to 8,000 / 90,000 / 1,200,000 SHU, plus a striped heat-warning marquee on the hottest — then a heat-philosophy manifesto, stockists and order section, and footer. Torn charred SVG edges divide sections. Copy is swaggering but craft-proud. Target 60fps: render the shader at 0.75× internal resolution, cap device pixel ratio at 2, pause everything offscreen, and serve a static magma frame under prefers-reduced-motion.

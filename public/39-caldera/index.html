The Foundry

Born in a burn barrel.
Raised on obsession.

CALDERA started as a mistake we refused to apologize for. A batch of fresnos left too long over a dying fire, skins blistered black, sugars caramelized to the edge of ruin. Everyone said throw it out. We bottled it. That bottle is why you're here.

Today we run a converted iron foundry on the edge of town — actual firebrick, actual flame, no steam-jacketed kettles pretending. Every pepper we melt is grown by farmers we know by first name, char-roasted in small cast batches of 300 bottles or fewer, and rested in oak like it earned it. Because it did.

We ferment slow, we char hard, and we never — ever — round the edges off. Extracts are for cowards. Additives are for accountants. If a batch doesn't glow, it doesn't ship.

Heat Philosophy

Heat is an ingredient.
Not a stunt.

Anyone can hurt you with an extract and a skull label. We're not interested. Heat should arrive like weather — you feel it building, it breaks, and it leaves the flavor standing.

TENET 01

Char is flavor's dark twin

Every pepper crosses live flame before it ferments. The blister carries smoke into the mash where a kettle never could. Burnt is a defect; charred is a decision.

TENET 02

Time does the heavy lifting

Ninety days in oak, minimum. Fermentation rounds capsaicin's spike into a slow rolling swell — the difference between a slap and a tide.

TENET 03

Print the real number

Every batch is lab-tested and the bottle wears its true Scoville figure. No "XXX", no "insanity" — a measurement. Fear the honest ones.

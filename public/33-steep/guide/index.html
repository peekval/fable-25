STEEP · Build notes

How the tea steeps

The hero is a small fluid illusion: no shaders, no physics library — one canvas, three thousand particles, and a field that never stops swirling.

How it was built

The dye is a pool of particles advected through a curl-noise velocity field — an analytic stream function whose curl guarantees divergence-free, swirling motion that reads as liquid rather than smoke. The canvas is never cleared; instead, every frame lays down a translucent wash of the cream background colour, so each particle's short stroke accumulates into long, soft diffusion trails that bloom and settle exactly like tea colouring still water.

Moving the cursor pours dye along the pointer's path, and the stroke's own momentum is handed to the new particles so a fast gesture flings colour before the field reclaims it. When the visitor is idle, a drip falls every few seconds from one of three drifting points, so the water is alive even untouched. The word STEEP sits inside the fluid: a blurred glyph mask is subtracted from the dye every frame, so the colour curls around and behind the letterforms instead of covering them. Everything runs in a single animation loop that pauses off-screen, caps device-pixel-ratio at two, halves the particle budget on mobile, and swaps to a pre-bloomed static swirl for reduced-motion visitors.

Generated assets

Between the ceremony timeline and the booking sits the page's one photographic breath: a portrait loop of steam rising from a matcha bowl in celadon glaze, resting on cream linen beside a bamboo whisk. The still was generated with Nano Banana 2 (Higgsfield, text-to-image, 2K), art-directed to the site's exact palette — celadon #B7C9B1, cream #F6F1E7, deep tea #4A3728, matcha #7A9B5E. Kling 3.0 Turbo (image-to-video, from that still) then set only the steam in motion while the bowl holds perfectly still.

The clip was finished with ffmpeg: a WebP poster cut from the source frame, h264 at CRF 26 for a ~164 KB file, and a crossfade of the clip's tail onto its own head so the ~4.5-second loop never visibly restarts. On the page it autoplays muted only while on screen — an IntersectionObserver pauses it the moment it scrolls away — and under prefers-reduced-motion it never plays at all; the matching poster simply holds the moment.

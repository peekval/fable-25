STEEP — a tea house for slow hours

A tea house for slow hours

Move your hand through the water — watch the leaf give itself away.

Philosophy

Tea is not brewed.
It is listened to.

Watch what happens when leaf meets water: nothing is forced. Colour finds its own way outward — a slow, certain unfurling that cannot be hurried and should not be. We built STEEP around that single observation.

Our studio holds six seats and one table of Swiss oak. There is no menu board, no queue, no music louder than the kettle. You sit, the water is brought to temperature, and for one hour the only task is to pay attention.

Every tea we pour is bought directly from four farms we visit each spring. Every cup is thrown by hand in a workshop two streets away. Everything else, we left out.

The pour

Three teas, chosen slowly

The list changes with the harvest, never with the season's fashion. Right now, we are pouring these.

The ceremony

One leaf, three minutes, undivided

Each seating follows the same quiet arc. The times below are not rules — they are the moments we have learned to wait for.

0:30

The awakening

The first water is poured off almost at once. It is not drunk — it only wakes the leaf, which begins to unfurl and remember its shape.

1:00

The first pour

Colour begins its slow descent through the glass. This cup is the brightest the leaf will ever be — we drink it without speaking.

2:00

Full expression

The leaf has given everything it held. What remains in the cup is structure, weight, and the long finish that follows you into the street.

Visit

Six seats.
One unhurried hour.

We seat twice each afternoon and once in the evening. Ceremonies last around seventy minutes and are held in near-silence — you are welcome exactly as you are.

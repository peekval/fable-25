STRATA

A fictional luxury residential tower where scrolling is the elevator: one sticky stage, four floors, a city that sinks as you rise, and floorplans that draw themselves at every stop.

IThe elevator scroll system

The ascent is a 560vh section wrapping a position:sticky, 100vh "cab". A single passive scroll listener computes progress p = (scrollY − top) / (height − vh) and hands it to one rAF-throttled render function. Everything is a pure function of p — transforms and opacity only, nothing re-layouts:

  • Parallax city: two inline-SVG skyline planes (dark rects, a few brass window pixels) sink at 0.55·vh and 1.25·vh with a haze gradient between them — the near plane drops below the fold by floor 24, which is what sells the altitude.
  • Ambient light: three stacked fixed-cost layers — warm street-glow radial (gone by p≈0.45), open dusk-sky gradient and a faint star field (fading in past p≈0.5) — cross-faded by opacity, never repainted.
  • Floor indicator: L · 12 · 24 · 38 · PH in brass Marcellus. The current stop is marked by a line-weight change (thicker, longer tick + brass color); a dot travels the hairline rail, eased between measured label centers, beside a continuous "ETAGE 07" readout. Under 720px the whole thing becomes a horizontal strip pinned to the top of the cab, ordered L→PH.
  • Stops: four residence tiers at progress centers ≈ .20/.44/.68/.92. Each card's translateY is tied to p − center so it physically rides up through the cab under a smoothstep opacity window; a huge stroked ghost numeral behind it names the floor at a glance.

IISelf-drawing floorplans

Each stop carries a hand-authored SVG plan. Every wall path gets pathLength="1", stroke-dasharray:1, stroke-dashoffset:1; when the stop goes live a class flips the offset to 0 and staggered transition-delays draw outer walls first, partitions next, brass loggia lines and door arcs last. Room labels fade in after the ink dries, and the m² figure counts up with an eased rAF loop. Leaving a stop resets the classes and the counter, so the plan redraws on every visit. The PH stop is the payoff: the penthouse photo wakes from brightness(.06) to full over ~2.6s with a diagonal light sweep — entering the room, not loading an image.

IIIAssets

Both photographs were generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP, palette-locked to the six site hexes (dusk navy #101823, brass #C7A867, cream stone #F2EEE6…): a 4:5 tower at blue hour with brass window glow, and a 16:9 penthouse interior at dusk. Upscaled to 2K, then converted with ffmpeg to WebP at the real rendered dimensions (1400×1750 and 1920×1080) — ~215 KB and ~110 KB. The tower loads eagerly as the hero; the penthouse is lazy.

IVQuiet-luxury discipline

  • Type: Marcellus for display and numerals, Archivo 300 for prose — no obvious Playfair/Cormorant pairing, no bold weights anywhere.
  • Brass is scarce: eyebrows, ticks, loggia strokes, door arcs, the m² digits. Walls draw in cream so plans read as ink on stone.
  • Reduced motion: the sticky stage collapses to static flow; stops stack as pre-composed blocks with plans fully drawn, counters set once, all parallax and glow layers removed. No rAF loop ever starts.
  • Performance: one scroll handler, contain:strict on the stage, transforms/opacity only, zero libraries, zero console errors from file://.

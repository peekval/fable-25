01 / PROCESS

FROM POWDER TO OBJECT

01

Dissolve

Every commission begins as a conversation and ends as a point cloud. We sculpt in simulation — gravity off, surface tension on — until the form holds itself.

CAD · implicit modelling

02

Deposit

A 400 W fibre laser fuses atomised metal powder in 30-micron passes. Eleven hours of light, drawn one cross-section at a time.

DMLS · 400 W · argon

03

Liberate

Wire EDM slices the piece from its build plate. The support lattice is broken away by hand; the scaffolding never survives the sculpture.

Wire EDM · heat relief

04

Mirror

Two days tumbling in ceramic media, then hand-burnishing to a liquid finish. Chrome is not a colour — it is everything standing around it.

Burnish · Ra < 0.05 µm

03 / MATERIAL

POWDER SCIENCE

Everything we print begins as gas-atomised powder: spheres of metal between 15 and 45 microns, each one a perfect casting of a droplet that froze in free fall.

Under the laser, a sphere lives for about 200 microseconds — solid, liquid, solid again. Do this four million times and you have a ring. The grain that results is finer than anything a foundry can pour, which is why a printed band survives at cross-sections a cast one cannot.

We keep our powder under argon, sieve it after every build, and retire it after twelve cycles. Metallurgy is mostly hygiene.

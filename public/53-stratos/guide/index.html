How the cabin window came alive.

The window system

The hero is a jet window built from real parts: a cream bezel (border-radius:44%/34%, inset highlights for moulded plastic), an inner pane with its own radius, a dust-seal ring, and a shade notch. The cloud loop (window.mp4, 188 KB) is injected by JS as autoplay muted loop playsinline with a poster; an IntersectionObserver pauses it offscreen, a decode error swaps in the still, and prefers-reduced-motion never creates the video at all — it gets the poster image.

What makes it feel inhabited is one custom property, --warm. A rAF loop (throttled to 4 Hz, gated on tab visibility and an IO on the hero) simulates a full day every 120 seconds: warmth peaks in gaussian bumps around sunrise and sunset, falls at night. That single number drives two soft-light tints on the glass (gold and slate blue), the ambient glow behind the hero, and a mono readout — local time, phase name, altitude and heading that jitter gently. The page breathes without a single keyframe.

The route ritual

The globe is a 2D canvas orthographic projection — no library. A dotted graticule is precomputed as unit vectors; each frame rotates them (longitude, then latitude), keeps the front hemisphere, and fades dots by depth. Routes are great circles via spherical interpolation (slerp) with a 4.5% altitude bulge. Selecting a route eases the view to the route’s midpoint (the latitude sign matters — pass 2 found it inverted), draws the arc on with a travelling dot and a flight-time counter, and refills the “your day, reclaimed” bars: STRATOS door-to-door vs airline, hours saved in the headline. The idle rotation is a ±0.07 rad sway, so a chosen route can never roll behind the horizon. DPR capped at 2, 45 fps cap, paused offscreen; reduced motion draws one static frame with every arc complete.

The descent

The last chapter is a 340vh wrapper with a sticky 100vh stage. Scroll progress (smoothstepped) crossfades a dawn gradient over the twilight, holds two blurred cream cloudbanks until 18%, then slides them off opposite corners while a thin veil dissolves and a flight-level readout counts FL450 down to arrival. Past 62% the CTA card “lands” — a settle keyframe with a small overshoot, like wheels kissing the runway. Reduced motion gets the arrived state: bright, clouds gone, no sticky.

The assets

Both images and the loop were generated with Nano Banana 2 and Kling 3.0 Turbo via the Higgsfield MCP, then finished with an ffmpeg pipeline. The wing-view still was prompted palette-exact (cabin cream, twilight blue, champagne sun on cloud tops), Kling animated it into a slow drift, and ffmpeg produced the deliverables: a 1280×720 h264 encode with a crossfaded head/tail so the ~5s clip self-loops seamlessly at 188 KB, plus WebP still and poster. The cabin interior is a second Nano Banana 2 render (1920×1080 WebP), revealed by a champagne light-sweep gradient animation the first time it scrolls into view.

Reproduction prompt

Build a one-page site for STRATOS, a fictional private-jet charter ("Time regained."), as a single self-contained HTML file. Quiet luxury at 45,000 ft: cabin cream #EDE6DA, twilight blue #1A2436 (deep #101826), champagne #D3B98A, walnut #6B4F38, cloud white #F8F5EF, dim #93887A. Fonts: Newsreader (light, optical sizing, italic accents) for display and body, Spline Sans Mono for kickers and instrument readouts. The page's arc is a night flight: dark twilight hero, deep-blue routes, walnut-and-cream cabin, cream service section, and an arrival that ends in daylight white.

Generate the assets first via the Higgsfield MCP: prompt Nano Banana 2 for a 16:9 view past a jet wing over an ocean of sunset cloud tops, champagne light on cream cloud, twilight blue sky above, palette-exact; feed it to Kling 3.0 Turbo image-to-video for an extreme-slow drift; finish with ffmpeg — crossfade the head and tail so the ~5s clip self-loops seamlessly, encode 1280x720 h264 CRF-high at under 200KB, and export a WebP still and poster. Prompt a second Nano Banana 2 render of the cabin: fourteen cream leather seats, walnut veneer, warm dusk light down the aisle, 1920x1080 WebP.

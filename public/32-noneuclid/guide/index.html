NON-EUCLID

HOW tHE IMPOSSIBLE BUILDINGS WERE RENDERED

01 how it was built

The hero is a single fragment shader doing real-time raymarching: for every pixel, a ray is stepped through a signed distance field (SDF) that reports how far the nearest surface is, and the ray safely jumps by that amount until it lands. The "buildings" are kaleidoscopic folded-space fractals — the field is mirrored, sorted, twisted and rescaled six times, so one cheap equation blooms into corridors, vaults and balconies with detail at every zoom level. There is no geometry, no mesh, no texture, and no image anywhere on the page.

Performance comes from honesty about what the eye can see: the canvas renders at roughly 0.65× internal resolution (0.5× on mobile) with device pixel ratio pinned to 1, then CSS upscales it — invisible under the heavy bone-white fog that also makes the fractal read as architecture rather than demoscene. Marching is capped at 64 steps (48 mobile) with a distance-scaled epsilon for free level-of-detail, and the loop fully stops when the tab is hidden or the hero scrolls away. The three structures are just three sets of four uniforms — scale, fold offset, twist, cap — and switching between them eases the numbers over 1.1 seconds, so one building visibly melts into the next.

02 the penrose moment

Section 03 stages a genuinely impossible object. Three beams with square cross-sections sit along the edges of a unit corner — mutually perpendicular, joined at only two elbows, with the two open ends a full body-diagonal apart. The trick is old and honest (Reutersvärd 1934, the Penroses 1958): both open ends lie on the line through the object's centre in the (1,1,1) direction, so a camera placed on that line sees the near end exactly occlude the far end and the tribar closes into the Penrose triangle.

The camera is driven only by scroll. Its spherical path (azimuth, elevation, roll) is split into two easeInOutCubic legs that meet precisely at the magic viewpoint — cubic easing has zero derivative at the joint, so angular velocity reaches zero exactly at the resolve and the sweet spot is wide in scroll space: the "aha" is earned, held, then deliberately broken. A near-orthographic projection (distance 16, focal 8.5, aspect-corrected) keeps the seam's perspective mismatch around 11% so the closure survives inspection. While disjoint, the three beams wear three different materials; as alignment approaches 1 they fuse into one bone-and-ultramarine structure with a signal-orange rim flare, and a HUD reports viewing alignment and flips to PARADOX RESOLVED. The same path rolls the camera's up-vector from −63° through 0° to +72° — a slow gravity shift that makes "down" leave with the paradox. The canvas renders only on scroll, resize or entry (no persistent loop), and under prefers-reduced-motion it collapses to a single static frame at the resolved angle with roll locked to zero.

03 the portal

The hero shader also carries a floating doorway. Before the march begins, the ray/portal-plane intersection is solved analytically; if the crossing lands inside the aperture, every step past that distance evaluates a second world — the same distance field rotated 90° and hung upside-down, shaded ink-dark with ultramarine light, orange rim and fog retargeted to near-black. Around the frame you see this space; through it, another. Two spaces, one door, zero extra render targets — the whole portal costs one branch per marching step.

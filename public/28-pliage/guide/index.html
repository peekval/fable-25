Le making-of

Comment ce site fut plié

No images, no libraries, no scissors. Une feuille de HTML, folded by hand — here is how, and here is the prompt to fold your own.

01How it was built

The hero is a real CSS 3D fold rig: a flat square sheet rendered in perspective, cut into four triangular corner flaps with clip-path, each flap’s transform-origin sitting exactly on its crease so the fold line becomes a physical hinge. Scrolling pins the scene and advances a step counter; each step rotates the next flap almost 180° about its own diagonal axis with a snappy ease, the way paper commits to a crease.

Every flap carries two faces — a near-white front and a back one luminance step darker — so a landed fold reads as paper turned against the light rather than a div turned in space. Dashed guide lines appear on the sheet just before each crease, a soft radial shadow sits on the floor plane beneath, and when the last corner closes, the packet dissolves and a flat-shaded low-poly crane rises and slowly beats its wings.

Everything moves on GPU-composited transforms only; a whisper of SVG turbulence lays paper grain over the whole page, and prefers-reduced-motion skips the choreography entirely and simply presents the finished crane.

Reproduction prompt — copy & fold

Build a one-page website for PLIAGE, a fictional Parisian origami atelier. Palette: paper white #FBF9F4, warm paper #F5F1E7, shadow gray #B8B2A6, one crane red #D9302F, graphite text #22201C. Fonts from Google: DM Serif Display for headlines, Nunito Sans for body. The signature is a 3D paper-folding hero built purely with CSS 3D transforms: a flat square sheet shown in perspective on a soft floor shadow, divided into four triangular corner flaps clipped along its diagonals. Each flap's transform-origin lies on its fold line and it rotates about that diagonal axis by nearly 180 degrees with a satisfying eased snap. Give every flap two backface-hidden faces - a near-white front and a back one luminance step darker - so folds read as paper catching light. Pin the scene and let scroll drive the choreography: each scrolled step triggers the next fold, with dashed guide lines appearing on the sheet just before each crease and a small red square at the sheet's heart that the folds swallow. After the final fold the packet dissolves and a flat-shaded low-poly SVG paper crane rises, gently bobbing and beating its wings. Sections: a folding hero with step captions, a philosophy-of-the-fold editorial with a pull quote and stats, a gallery of three slowly rotating polygon sculptures (crane, boat, fox) on cards with page-curl hover corners, atelier workshops on a graphite panel, and a footer. Add subtle SVG-turbulence paper grain, refined bilingual French-accented copy, and show the finished crane immediately under prefers-reduced-motion.

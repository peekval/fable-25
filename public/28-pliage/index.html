Le pli, pas à pas — the fold, step by step

Atelier de papier — Paris IIIe — depuis MMXI

PLIAGE

Une feuille, zéro ciseaux. One square of paper, folded until it remembers being a bird.

Faites défiler pour plier
pli 00 — 04

La feuille

Un carré de washi, 21 cm. Every crane begins as a field — flat, patient, undecided. The first guide already whispers across the grain.

pli 01 — 04

Premier pli

The north-west corner folds to the heart. Paper never bends — it decides. One eased snap, et voilà: light falls one tone darker on the folded face.

pli 02 — 04

Les ailes dormantes

Two corners fold in together, a mirrored pair, one breath apart. Origami is symmetry you can almost hear.

pli 03 — 04

Le paquet

The last corner closes the blintz. Le cœur rouge disparaît — folded inside, where it stays. Less paper; more memory.

pli 04 — 04

L’envol

Sixteen creases we did not show you happen here, entre deux battements. The sheet forgets it was flat. La grue remembers everything else.

Notre credo

La philosophie du pli

Nous ne coupons jamais. We never cut. A pair of scissors solves a problem; a fold keeps it — pressed flat, carried forward, visible forever in the finished bird.

Chaque pli est une décision. Make it slowly, make it once. The paper will hold your hesitation as faithfully as your confidence, and it will show both to anyone who looks along the light.

« Le papier n’oublie jamais son premier pli. » Paper never forgets its first fold — maxim of the atelier

We fold on a long oak table under north light, because north light does not flatter — it tells the truth about a crease. Nos élèves apprennent d’abord à attendre: to wait for the fibre to settle before the next fold.

The washi comes from a single mill we will not name. It arrives by post on Tuesdays, wrapped in itself — naturally.

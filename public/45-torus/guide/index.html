FABLE/25 · Site 45 · Build guide

How the star got held.

One HTML file, one 2D canvas, three generated assets. The whole site is built around a single earned moment: scrolling a fusion reactor through its real startup sequence until it ignites.

01The ignition scrub

Hero and ignition chapter share one 560vh section with a sticky full-viewport canvas. A scroll listener does nothing but compute progress p = -rect.top / (height - viewport); every visual is a pure function of p, which is what makes the sequence perfectly reversible when you scroll back up.

  • Status lines type in by slicing each string to floor(subProgress × length) characters inside a per-line progress window — no timers, so reverse-scrolling untypes them.
  • Plasma intensity is a smoothstep ramp through the stages plus a sharp extra step at p ≈ 0.82 — the ignition jump.
  • The Q-factor readout is tuned to cross exactly 1.0 at the IGNITION line (the physics punchline: net gain), then runs away to 11.3.
  • The shockglow is a fixed radial-gradient overlay whose opacity is a Gaussian bump centred just past ignition plus a settling plateau — a slow flash that washes the section and stays as an afterglow.

02The plasma torus (canvas 2D, no libraries)

The torus is parametric: a point at toroidal angle θ and poloidal angle φ lands at (R + r·cosφ)·(cosθ, sinθ·squash) minus a vertical tube offset. ~900 particles (340 under 768px) each carry their own angular speeds and are drawn as short streaks along their direction of motion with globalCompositeOperation = "lighter", so overlapping streams add up to glow. Fourteen thin poloidal ellipses hug the tube as magnetic field lines; their radius shrinks as intensity rises — the field visibly tightens its grip. Colour lerps from magenta #E24FBB through violet #8E4FE2, and particles flagged as core-bound whiten toward #FFB37A only above 55% intensity. DPR is capped at 2, and an IntersectionObserver plus visibilitychange stop the rAF loop whenever the stage is offscreen or the tab hidden.

03The assets — inside the vessel

The porthole footage is generated, not stock: a Nano Banana 2 still of a tokamak interior mid-shot (swirling magenta-violet plasma ring, palette-exact) was fed to Kling 3.0 Turbo image-to-video via the Higgsfield MCP for a slow plasma churn, then finished with ffmpeg: a 1920-wide WebP still, a matching poster frame, and an h264 CRF encode whose head and tail are crossfaded so the ~4.5 s clip self-loops seamlessly at ~430 KB. On the page it's framed as CAM-04 — a live camera feed with corner ticks, REC dot and shot metadata. The video element is injected by JS only when motion is allowed (autoplay muted loop playsinline preload="metadata", poster set), and an IntersectionObserver pauses it offscreen.

04Accessibility & the reduced-motion cut

prefers-reduced-motion gets a different edit, not a broken one: the scrub section collapses to a single viewport showing one static post-ignition frame, the HUD renders fully typed with Q at 11.3, the porthole shows the poster still, and reveals/counters resolve instantly. Fonts: Unbounded (monumental display) + Martian Mono (technical everything-else).

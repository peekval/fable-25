SINGULAR — The last thing light does

How this experience works — and the prompt that rebuilds it. All of it is code: no image or audio assets, everything procedural.

01 The hole

A raw-WebGL fragment shader bends a procedural starfield around the shadow with a screen-space lensing field; the accretion disk is doppler-shifted — one side blazing blue-white, the other dim red — under a thin photon ring. Lean shader, reduced internal resolution, static-frame degrade on weak rasterizers.

02 The fall

Scroll chapters approach: Orbit (calm facts) -> Approach (lensing intensifies) -> Spaghettification, where the typography itself stretches and tears apart with tidal force -> the horizon, where the screen swallows to black -> Beyond: a pure-white, perfectly still chapter. The contrast is the payoff.

03 Time dilation

Two clocks run from the start: proper time keeps ticking, observer time slows and freezes at the horizon. 'To everyone watching, you never fell.'

04 Fallbacks

No-WebGL gets an elegant canvas disk; reduced motion serves readable chapters with mild fixed stretch; the loop pauses offscreen and on hidden tabs.

05 Probe VII — generated footage as a scroll scrubber

The one generated asset: a Kling 3.0 Turbo flight toward the hole (from a Higgsfield text-to-video prompt), exploded into 40 WebP frames with ffmpeg and scrubbed on a canvas by scroll position — damped playhead, lazy-loaded via IntersectionObserver, last frame as the reduced-motion still. Your scroll is her playhead.

Reproduction prompt
Build a single-file black-hole experience called "SINGULAR — The last thing light does." Void black (#05060A), disk gradient white-blue -> amber-red (#FFB37A), monumental grotesque + mono. Hero: a raw-WebGL black hole — fragment shader with screen-space gravitational lensing warping a procedural starfield around the shadow, a doppler-shifted accretion disk (approaching side brighter and bluer, receding side dim red), and a thin photon ring; keep the shader lean, render at ~0.6x internal resolution, and degrade to a static rendered frame if frame times explode. Scroll is the fall, in chapters: ORBIT (safe distance; calm mono facts: mass, spin), APPROACH (lensing and disk grow), SPAGHETTIFICATION (headlines stretch vertically and letter-spacing tears words apart progressively with tidal force — legible until deliberately not), THE HORIZON (the screen swallows to black over one long beat) and BEYOND (a pure-white inverted chapter, perfectly still: "Inside, all directions point to the center."), ending on a single point. Run two clocks from the start: proper time ticks steadily while observer time slows and freezes at the horizon, with physics-poetic captions ("To everyone watching, you never fell."). prefers-reduced-motion: static hole, mild fixed type-stretch, all chapters readable. No-WebGL: elegant canvas/CSS disk. Pause offscreen/hidden; DPR cap 1.5; watch overflow during the stretch (clip it); no horizontal overflow at 390px; zero console errors. Add one generated asset: a Kling 3.0 Turbo text-to-video flight toward the hole, extracted to ~40 WebP frames (ffmpeg fps=8) and scroll-scrubbed on a 16:9 canvas panel framed as "PROBE VII — FINAL TRANSMISSION" with a frame readout — damped playhead (lerp ~0.28), IntersectionObserver lazy-load, reduced motion shows the final frame.

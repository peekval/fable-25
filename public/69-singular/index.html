01 · ORBIT — r = 1,200 rs

A safe distance

From out here it is almost modest: a coin of missing sky, ringed by a light that never quite touches it. Four million suns, folded into a region smaller than Mercury's orbit, holding this entire galaxy in a slow rotation around itself.

Nothing about it is visible. Everything about it is inferred — from the way light behaves in the last moments before it is lost.

02 · APPROACH — r = 40 rs

The disk resolves

The stars behind it are sliding out of the way now — dragged into arcs around the shadow, doubled, smeared. Gravity is a lens with no glass in it.

The accretion disk orbits at nearly half the speed of light, and you can see the speed directly: the limb racing toward you is relativistically beamed — brighter, hotter, blue-white. The limb fleeing you is dimmed and stretched red. One object, two colors. That asymmetry is not a rendering choice. It is special relativity, visible to the naked eye.

Above and below the shadow, a thin second image of the disk: light from the far side, bent a full half-turn over the top to reach you. You are seeing behind the thing.

03 · r = 6 rs — tidal shear rising

Tidal
Forces

Gravity is not the problem. The difference in gravity is. Your feet are closer than your head, so your feet fall harder — and the disagreement between the two becomes a force that pulls along your spine.

For a hole this massive, the tide at the horizon is survivably gentle — you would cross without noticing. The typography exaggerates. So did every physicist who ever named this process "spaghettification" and then kept a straight face.

04 · r = 1 rs — event horizon

Nothing marks it

The horizon is not a surface. There is no membrane, no flash, no toll. It is only the radius where every future path — every one — bends inward. You cross it in perfect silence, mid-thought,

and the sky behind you closes like an eye.

05 · INSIDE

Inside, all directions point to the center.

There is no way out, because "out" is no longer a direction that exists — the way there is no path on Earth that leads to yesterday.

The singularity is not a place ahead of you. It is a moment ahead of you. It sits in your future the way tomorrow does, and it arrives the same way: calmly, and no matter what you do.

Here, your clock is the only one left. It reads one second per second. It always did.

How WOBBLE got its jiggle

The hero is now a bowl of real soft-body WebGL gummies — poke one and it squishes, squints and puffs sugar dust. Below the fold it’s still divs doing cartoon physics in pure CSS. Here’s the recipe, and the prompt that rebuilds it.

How it was built

The hero gummies are soft-body blobs in three.js (r147, loaded async from a CDN) wearing one custom shader. The vertex shader keeps them alive: idle breathing, a faint surface ripple, a traveling wobble ring that radiates from the last poke and decays in space and time, spring-driven squash along the poke axis with a volume-preserving bulge, and velocity stretch while flung. The fragment shader fakes edible translucency — depth-tinted transmission (thick center chews deep, thin rim goes light), a tinted fresnel rim whose alpha drops so edges go see-through, twin wet speculars, broad sheen, a backlight glow — plus a squinty little face that fades in on poked blobs. CPU-side damped springs drive position and squash, so a click squishes with overshoot, a drag flings with a settle wobble, and sugar-dust particles (THREE.Points) puff on impact. DPR is capped at 2, the rAF loop pauses offscreen and on hidden tabs, small screens get three lower-poly blobs, and if WebGL or the CDN is missing the original CSS candy divs simply stay.

Everything else keeps the CSS squash-and-stretch language on one springy curve, cubic-bezier(.34,1.86,.44,1) — hero letters drop and squash on landing, jelly buttons compress on press — but every loop is now transform/opacity-only: the old border-radius morphs and SVG d:path() wave animations repainted gradients and shadows every frame (~17fps), so the candies wobble via rotate/scale and the wave dividers drift a double-width path with translateX. The dark flavor lab still bursts fourteen confetti dots per press and unlocks milestone microcopy at 10, 25 and 50 wobbles. Reduced-motion visitors get static gummies, rendered once, with no jiggle loop.

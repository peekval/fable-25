Maree · build notes · N°49

How a page sails from golden hour into night.

The living ocean

The hero is one 2D canvas painted as a sine-sum sea: ~44 horizontal wave rows (30 on mobile), spaced with a power curve so they compress toward the horizon like real perspective. Each row's height is a sum of four sines with mismatched frequencies, speeds and phases — cheap Gerstner flavour that never repeats visibly. A gradient sky, a sun disc with radial halo, and a specular gold column are painted first; then each row is stroked twice — once in pale blue-grey, and again in short champagne segments wherever a crest rises inside the widening sun-path cone, with flicker noise so the glitter shivers. Near rows spawn spray particles at high crests (gravity, one-second lives). Pointer moves push damped radial ripples into the row math. The loop is paused by an IntersectionObserver when the hero leaves the viewport and by visibilitychange when the tab hides; DPR is capped at 2.

The voyage scroll

A 420vh wrapper with a sticky 100svh stage. Scroll progress cross-fades three stacked sky layers (golden → dusk → night) with smoothstep windows while a pure-gradient sun sinks below the sea line and 110 DOM stars fade in. In the chart card, a gold course line (SVG stroke-dashoffset) draws from Marseille to Calvi across a graticule with mono lat/long labels, waypoints popping as the vessel dot passes; heading, speed-over-ground and the ship's clock (17:55 → 06:31) interpolate with the same progress. Six log entries type themselves in when the night reaches their timestamp, caret and all.

The shipyard reveal

The naval-architecture section is a 300vh sticky figure. Hand-drawn SVG hull lines — profile with sheer, keel fin, rudder and rig, four waterlines, and a nested body plan — are measured with getTotalLength() and drawn by scroll-driven stroke-dashoffset with per-path stagger while a "% faired" readout counts up. Past 58% progress, nine horizontal "planks" (strips of the yacht photo, each a full-height background offset into its slice) slide in alternately from both edges with cubic easing until the drawing has planked over into the golden-hour photograph.

Generated assets

The photography is generated: a palette-exact golden-hour still of the navy-hulled sloop (navy #0E1B2A hull, teak deck, champagne light) was created with Nano Banana 2, then fed to Kling 3.0 Turbo image-to-video — both driven through the Higgsfield MCP — for the bow cutting swell. An ffmpeg pipeline finished it: a 1920-wide WebP hero still, a 960×540 poster, and an h264 CRF-26 encode with crossfaded head/tail so the ~508KB clip loops seamlessly. On the page the loop is injected by JS only when motion is allowed (autoplay muted loop playsinline, poster, preload=metadata), paused offscreen by an IntersectionObserver; reduced-motion users get the poster only — and, in the hero, the still photograph instead of the canvas sea.

Reproduction prompt

Local assets in assets/: yacht.webp (1920×1080 golden-hour hero photo), bow.mp4 (960×540 seamless loop of the bow cutting swell, ~500KB) and bow-poster.webp. Generate them first: prompt Nano Banana 2 for a palette-exact golden-hour aerial of a modern 28 m sloop — deep navy hull #0E1B2A, teak deck, white sails, sun low on the horizon — then feed the still to Kling 3.0 Turbo image-to-video via the Higgsfield MCP for the bow-cutting-swell motion, and finish with ffmpeg: 1920-wide WebP still, 960×540 poster, h264 CRF-26 with crossfaded head/tail so the clip self-loops. Palette: navy sea #0E1B2A (deep #091320), champagne gold #D8B77A, foam white #F4F1EA, teak #A97B4F, dim #6E7E90. Fonts: Cormorant Garamond display (italic gold accents), Crimson Pro body, IBM Plex Mono for instrument labels. Hero: a living canvas ocean — ~44 sine-sum wave rows compressed toward the horizon with perspective, gradient dusk sky, sun halo and a specular gold column, champagne glitter re-stroked on crests inside the widening sun path, spray particles spawning at near-row crests, pointer ripples fed into the row math; IntersectionObserver + visibilitychange pause the loop, DPR capped at 2. Then a 420vh voyage section with a sticky stage: scroll cross-fades golden → dusk → night skies, a pure-gradient sun sinks, 110 stars fade in, and a chart card draws a gold course line (stroke-dashoffset) from Marseille to Calvi across a graticule with mono lat/long labels, popping waypoints, live heading/SOG/clock readouts, and a ship's log that types itself entry by entry ("18:40 — wind 14 kn, heading 214°"). Follow with a full-bleed under-sail video loop (JS-injected only when motion allowed, autoplay muted loop playsinline, poster, IO-paused offscreen). Then a 300vh sticky naval-architecture figure: SVG hull lines — profile, sheer, keel, rudder, rig, waterlines, nested body plan — drawing via measured stroke-dashoffset with stagger and a "% faired" counter, then nine photo planks sliding in alternately from both edges until the drawing planks over into yacht.webp. Finish with a yard section (craft copy, four IO-triggered counters) and a commission CTA.

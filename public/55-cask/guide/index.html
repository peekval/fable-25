The whisky that ages
as you scroll.

How CASK No. 9 works: a scroll-driven aging system inside an SVG cask cutaway, a canvas of peat smoke, and a lateral walk through Warehouse No. 4 — all in one self-contained HTML file.

01 — The aging interpolation systemTwenty-five years in 540vh

The years chapter is a 540vh section with a sticky 100svh stage. Scroll progress p maps linearly to year = p × 25, and one function, setYear(y), drives everything:

  • Colour ramp. Seven keyframes — [year, r, g, b, alpha] — run from pale new-make (244,234,192 @ .30) through #E8A94E and #B5651D to deep mahogany (62,28,10 @ .97). The active pair is linearly interpolated and written to three SVG gradient stops: the top stop brightened ×1.22, the bottom darkened ×0.62, so the liquid always reads as lit from within.
  • The angels’ share. Fill level is 1 − 0.011×year (about 2% by volume feel, ~72.5% at 25). The liquid lives in a nested <svg> whose y/height are set per frame — a nested svg clips to its own viewport, giving a perfect rectangular liquid window inside the cavity clipPath, no recomputed paths. A meniscus group translates with the surface; an italic “the angels’ share” label fades in as the headspace opens.
  • Vintage notes. Eight tasting notes carry data-y (vanilla at 5, dried fruit at 12, leather at 20…). Notes at or below the current year get .lit; the newest gets .current and an amber rule. On mobile only the current note shows, as a bottom ribbon.
  • Side effects. ABV decays 63.5% → 54.8%; the ambient glow behind the cask reads its hue from CSS variables --liq-r/g/b set from the same ramp.

The caustic shimmer is three blurred warm ellipses on mix-blend-mode:screen plus a radial “backlit” glint band, animated by CSS keyframes — zero JS cost, frozen automatically under reduced motion.

02 — Peat smokeWisps on canvas

Two canvases (hero, warehouse) share one Smoke engine: ~30 soft radial-gradient blobs steered by a cheap pseudo-curl field (sin/cos sums over position and time) so they drift upward and coil rather than float straight. Fast scrolling feeds a stir impulse (decaying ×0.94 per frame) into both drift speed and lift, so the smoke visibly swirls when you rush it. DPR is capped at 2; an IntersectionObserver plus visibilitychange stop each rAF loop the moment its canvas is off-screen or the tab hides.

03 — The warehouse walkLateral pan to your cask

A 340vh section with a sticky stage: the warehouse photo sits in a 172%-wide wrapper translated by -panMax × ease (ease-out ^1.55 — the walk slows as you arrive). A stencil counter ticks CASK 0001 → 0247 with the pan; past 80% it flips to CASK Nº 9 — yours, if you like, the private-cask card slides in, and a one-shot class fires the wax-seal stamp: scale 2.6 → 0.92 → 1.05 → 1 with blur decay, rotated −8°, like a fist coming down on hot wax.

04 — AssetsNano Banana 2 → ffmpeg WebP

Pipeline Both photographs were generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP, palette-locked to the site (#1C1410 shadows, #E8A94E highlights), then encoded with ffmpeg to WebP at real render sizes: warehouse.webp 1920×1072 (~185 KB, dunnage warehouse with one window shaft) and dram.webp 1400×1738 (~187 KB, backlit crystal tumbler on wet slate). Both loading="lazy" with width/height attributes.

The dram gets a light-raise reveal: it renders at brightness(.28) and transitions to full brightness over 1.5s when 45% visible — someone switching the lamp on behind the glass.

05 — RobustnessReduced motion & mobile

prefers-reduced-motion collapses both scroll sections to static single screens pinned at the 18-year state (counter 18, liquid at its 18-year hue and level, notes to 18 lit, seal already stamped); both canvases are removed and the pan is neutralised. Mobile swaps the three-column stage for counter / cask / note-ribbon rows; every overflowing effect is clipped at section level, so scrollWidth stays ≤ 390 at 390px.

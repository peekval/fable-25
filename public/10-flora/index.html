The Ritual

Three gestures,
morning and night.

Good skin is not a purchase; it is a habit with a scent. Ours takes four minutes and asks only that you slow down for them.

No. 01 — Cleanse

Warm water, soft cloth

Begin with water just warmer than skin. A muslin cloth and thirty unhurried seconds lift the day away without stripping what your skin made to protect itself.

No. 02 — Feed

Three drops, pressed in

While skin is still damp, press — don't rub — three drops of essence into cheeks, brow, and throat. Damp skin drinks; dry skin merely wears.

No. 03 — Seal

Oil against the evening

Finish with a thin veil of oil, warmed between palms. It holds the water in and the weather out — a coat for your face, cut from a meadow.

30 May · Harvest

Calendula at first light

The resin that makes calendula worth growing sits highest in the flower before the dew burns off. So we pick at six, gloves sticky by seven, and the whole drying shed smells of honey and pepper by noon.

18 April · Pressing

Why the oil runs deeper each autumn

Batch 41 of Amber Root came out nearly brick-red. Nothing is wrong — late-season sea buckthorn simply carries more carotenoids. Your bottle is darker because the berries had a longer summer. We think that's worth keeping.

2 March · Method

A short defence of slow maceration

You can extract a flower in an hour with heat, or in five weeks with patience. Heat gets you more volume and less of everything else. We tried both, sent them to the lab twice, and chose the five weeks. Every time.

TERRA NOVA — how it was built

One icosphere, two shells, and a slider that plays four hundred years in four seconds.

THE TECHNIQUE

The planet is a single icosphere whose surface is displaced in the vertex shader by multi-octave simplex noise — continents, ridgelines and detail are all math, no textures, no images. A custom fragment shader colors each point by elevation, and per-vertex finite differences on the same noise field give the terrain real lighting. The entire terraforming morph is driven by a handful of shader uniforms: as the TERRAFORM value rises, a sea-level threshold climbs and flattens the lowlands into specular ocean, land ramps lerp from rust to green, and a second shell fades in animated noise as cloud cover. A slightly larger back-side sphere runs an additive fresnel shader for the atmosphere rim glow, widening and shifting from dust-haze to cyan as the air thickens. Because nothing is ever rebuilt — no geometry, no materials — the whole transformation costs the same as drawing the planet once, and the slider, the scroll position and every data readout stay locked to a single damped value.

GENERATED ASSETS

The page carries exactly one image: the Sol 1147 surface survey, assets/surface.webp (1920×814, 21:9). It was generated with Nano Banana 2 (via the Higgsfield MCP, text-to-image at 2K) from a palette-locked prompt — the model was given the site's exact colors (void #05060B sky, barren rust #B0552F plains, pioneer green #4CAF6E growth, cyan #7FD4E8 atmospheric haze) and asked for a ground-level panorama from a ridge on a half-terraformed world: rust to the west, four-year-old cyanobacterial green to the east, a lone weather-station mast on the slope. The 2K output was compressed to WebP with ffmpeg.

Its reveal is staged as a telemetry-scan: a glowing cyan scanline sweeps the frame left to right in lockstep with a linear CSS clip-path wipe, uncovering the photograph as heat-haze — blurred and oversaturated — which settles into clarity only after the beam clears the frame. A survey-grid overlay, corner registration brackets and mono coordinate/timestamp chips then fade in, framing it as mission-archive material, and a readout tape beneath ticks O₂ (ppm), surface temperature and pressure off the same damped terraform value that drives the planet's shader uniforms. Reduced-motion visitors get the finished, unblurred photograph with the HUD already in place.

REPRODUCTION PROMPT

Prompt

Build a single-page dark sci-fi showcase called TERRA NOVA, a hopeful exoplanet terraforming program. Palette: void #05060B background, barren rust #B0552F, terraformed green #4CAF6E, atmosphere cyan #7FD4E8, bone #EDEBE4 text. Fonts: Krona One for display headings, Inter for UI and body. The persistent hero is a full-viewport three.js procedural planet: an icosphere displaced in the vertex shader by multi-octave simplex noise, colored in the fragment shader by elevation, with finite-difference normals for lighting. Wrap it in a slightly larger back-side sphere running an additive fresnel shader for atmosphere rim glow, plus a cloud shell whose alpha comes from animated noise. The showstopper: a TERRAFORM slider synced to scroll progress that morphs the planet purely through shader uniforms — land colors lerp rust to green, a rising sea level flattens low terrain into flat specular ocean, cloud cover thickens, and the rim glow widens and shifts from dust-haze to cyan. Data readouts (atmosphere pressure, oxygen, ocean coverage, temperature) count up in sync with the morph. Static point starfield behind. One generated photograph: a 21:9 ground-level panorama of the half-terraformed surface (palette-locked text-to-image, Nano Banana 2 at 2K, ffmpeg → WebP) presented full-bleed as "SOL 1147 — first surface survey" mission-archive material — survey grid overlay, corner brackets, mono coordinates and timestamp — revealed by a cyan telemetry scanline moving in lockstep with a linear clip-path wipe, heat-haze blur settling to clarity after the beam passes, with an O₂/temperature/pressure tape beneath ticking off the same damped terraform value. Sections: mission statement, four-phase timeline with live progress bars, the surface survey panorama, biosphere telemetry grid, join-the-program application, footer. Mood: mission-control minimalism, visionary but grounded, generous letterspacing, thin hairline borders. Keep 60fps: no geometry rebuilds, device pixel ratio capped at 2, lower sphere detail on mobile, pause when hidden, and honor prefers-reduced-motion with a static planet at 50% terraform and instant slider steps.

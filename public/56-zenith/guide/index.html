Scrolling is the flight.

ZENITH is a fictional civilian suborbital company, and the page is its whole mission profile: dawn runway at the top, the black of space in the middle, the same desert floor at the bottom. Eleven minutes, one scrollbar.

01The ascent ramp system

Everything on the page is driven by one number: altitude. Invisible <span data-key> markers sit inside the copy at each mission phase, each carrying {t, alt, vel, g, glow} — mission time, altitude, velocity, cabin load, re-entry glow. On scroll, a single rAF-throttled sampler finds the two markers bracketing the viewport anchor (55% down the screen) and linearly interpolates all five channels. That one sampled state feeds:

  • the fixed sky layer — a two-stop gradient interpolated through a 7-stop colour ramp, dawn #DCE6F0 → stratosphere #7FA3CC → space #05070C;
  • the HUD (ALT / MET / VEL / CABIN) and the left altitude rail, 0→100 km with the Kármán line marked in telemetry green;
  • a star canvas (DPR capped at 2) whose global alpha is a smoothstep of altitude between 55 and 85 km — stars literally fade in as the air runs out;
  • a gold sun-disc/horizon wash that dies by 16 km and returns on landing;
  • an amber re-entry overlay keyed to the glow channel.

G-force typography: Archivo is a variable font with real wdth/wght axes, so cabin load is typographic load — during boost the ascent headlines compress from width 112 to ~79 and gain ~250 weight units at 3.6 g, and the mission clock digits shudder (a 90 ms steps() jitter) only above 3.25 g, only during boost.

02The capsule window

At apogee the page stops narrating and shows you. A 300vh sticky stage holds the generated capsule photo full-viewport behind a clip-path: inset(… round 30% 42%) mask — a rounded capsule-window geometry that eases open from a small centered pane to the full screen while the image settles from scale 1.16 to 1. A thin telemetry frame (corner brackets, ALT 104.2 KM / VEL 0012 / CABIN 0.00 G) fades in over the last third, and a single caption earns its capitals: THIS IS THE WHOLE POINT.

03Weightless UI

For the apogee chapter only, the interface goes off-structure: crew-note cards drift on slow 8–12 s alternating keyframe loops (a few px of translate, ~1° of rotation, mismatched negative delays so nothing syncs), gated by an IntersectionObserver so they only animate in view. Descent re-grounds everything — the same cards' siblings in the return chapter sit still, the sky ramp runs in reverse, and the re-entry glow gives one honest amber flare at 4.2 g. prefers-reduced-motion collapses the whole system to its calmest true state: window fully open, no float, no shudder, no twinkle; the HUD still reads correctly.

04The asset

NANO BANANA 2HIGGSFIELD MCP FFMPEG → WEBP

One image carries the emotional peak: assets/capsule.webp (1920×1072, ~158 KB). Generated with Nano Banana 2 via the Higgsfield MCP — a white civilian capsule above the Earth's curve at apogee, crew warm-lit through the cabin glass, black star-flecked space above, the thin blue atmosphere limb below — then converted and compressed to WebP with ffmpeg. It loads lazily with real dimensions and is revealed only through the window mask, so the page never pays for it until the flight does.

05Reproduction prompt

Build a one-page site for ZENITH, a fictional civilian suborbital spaceflight company, as a single self-contained HTML file (Google Fonts allowed; one local asset: assets/capsule.webp, 1920×1072). Tagline: "Eleven minutes above the world." Palette: dawn sky #DCE6F0 → stratosphere #7FA3CC → space black #05070C as an ascent colour ramp, plus capsule white #F4F6F8, sun gold #E8B84A, telemetry green #59D98C, ink #10151C. Fonts: Archivo variable (wdth 62–125, wght 200–900) for a confident aerospace grotesque, JetBrains Mono for telemetry.

Make scrolling BE the flight. Place invisible span markers through the copy carrying {mission time, altitude, velocity, cabin g, re-entry glow}; on scroll, sample the two markers around a 55%-viewport anchor and interpolate all channels in one rAF update. Drive from that single state: a fixed sky gradient interpolated through a 7-stop ramp from dawn blue to space black; a fixed HUD top-right (ALT / MET clock from T−10:00 to T+11:04 / VEL / CABIN g); a left altitude rail 0–100 km with JS-generated ticks, a gold cursor, and the Kármán line marked in telemetry green; a DPR≤2 star canvas whose alpha smoothsteps in between 55 and 85 km, twinkling only while visible and the tab focused; a gold sun-disc horizon glow that fades by 16 km; an amber overlay keyed to the glow channel for re-entry.

Sections as mission phases: PRE-FLIGHT (dawn, a quiet promise, a checklist card ending "YOUR HEART RATE — ELEVATED … that's correct"); ASCENT with IGNITION, MAX-Q and MECO copy blocks — during boost, headlines physically load up via Archivo's axes (width 112→~79, weight +250 at 3.6 g) and the clock digits shudder above 3.25 g; a KÁRMÁN LINE moment (a telemetry-green rule that draws itself at 100 km); APOGEE as a 300vh sticky stage where the capsule photo fills the viewport behind an expanding capsule-window clip-path (inset … round, easing from a small centred pane to full bleed, image scale 1.16→1) framed by thin corner brackets and HUD readouts, captioned "THIS IS THE WHOLE POINT."; a WEIGHTLESSNESS chapter titled "Unbuckle." where crew-note cards float freely (slow alternating drift/rotation loops, staggered negative delays, IntersectionObserver-gated); RETURN (re-entry interface with the amber glow and 4.2 g, drogues/mains, touchdown at T+11:04 back in dawn light); YOUR SEAT (three training days, $450,000 priced with dignity — "we'd rather be honest than popular" — refundable deposits, seat-briefing CTA).

How Field Notes was drawn

How it was built

Every illustration on the sketchbook — the suitcase, the tram, the tiles, the lighthouse — is hand-plotted inline SVG, and each stroke carries pathLength="1" with a dash offset so it can draw itself in when scrolled into view. A small script measures every path’s real length with getTotalLength, gives long strokes more time than short ones, and compresses the whole choreography so no page takes more than about four seconds to finish.

Colour arrives late on purpose: soft ellipse washes, dashed route lines and handwritten labels fade in only after the ink is down, exactly like a real sketchbook session. The paper itself is layered CSS — rotated sheets, ruled and dotted notebook backgrounds, washi tape cut with clip-path, a corner-curl shadow — under a full-page SVG turbulence grain. A pooled set of fading dots trails the cursor like pencil dust, and without JavaScript or with reduced motion, every drawing simply appears fully finished.

Three finished paintings are now taped and paper-clipped into the pages — tram 28 in Alfama, an azulejo corner study with margin swatch dabs, and a fishing harbour at golden hour. Each washes in when scrolled into view: the image sits behind a stack of four soft-edged radial-gradient CSS mask layers whose mask-size keyframes from 0% to 340% at staggered rates while saturation and a slight blur resolve, so the picture spreads like pigment on wet paper; a handwritten Caveat caption then writes itself in with a clip-path wipe and a self-drawing pencil underline. Hovering lifts a painting — it straightens, rises and its shadow deepens, as if picked up for a closer look — and the sheets and plates drift at slightly different scroll rates (the CSS translate property, so the resting rotations survive). Reduced motion shows every painting pre-revealed with no parallax.

Generated assets

The three watercolours (tram.webp, azulejo.webp, harbor.webp) were generated with Nano Banana 2 through the Higgsfield MCP at 2K, from watercolour-style prompts locked to the site palette — paper #F8F4EA, ink #3A3733, blue #2B4C7E, terracotta #D97D54, olive #8A9B6E, sky #7FA8C9 — then converted and optimised to WebP with ffmpeg. They load lazily with real width/height attributes so the sketchbook never reflows.
